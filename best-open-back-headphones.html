The Best Open-Back Headphones

May 2024 · 11 minute read

Open-back headphones let you enjoy your music in a whole new light. With their open-ear design, they allow air and sound to pass through the ear cups at the rear of the speaker driver.

This results in a more natural sound output that is much better suited to critical listening. And because the sounds around you are incorporated into the audio, it creates a more immersive experience, as if the sound were being presented in a live setting.

Often favored by professionals and audiophiles alike, open-back headphones are ideal for anybody who wants to hear every detail their music has to offer.

Choosing the Best Pair of Open-Back Headphones for You

Open-backed headphones allow you to experience your music with exceptional depth. By mixing in the ambient sounds around you, rather than canceling them out, they engineer a level of immersion that closed-back headphones cannot achieve.

The illusion created by open-backed headphones is that your audio becomes a living, breathing thing, that interacts fluidly with the world around you. Sounds are rendered with pinpoint precision, and you are provided with a more natural sound stage, and more expansive audio.

On the downside, open-back headphones do allow for considerable noise leakage, so are probably best enjoyed away from your daily commute, or the office.

If you're willing to splash the cash, the Sennheiser HD 800 S is one of the very best premium open-back headphones you can get. With reference-grade audiophile sound quality, vibration damping, and luxurious comfort, they offer one of the best seats in the house for those who can afford to succumb to their charms.

On the other hand, if you have a more modest budget, we recommend the HIFIMAN HE400SE Stealth Magnets, which represent a much more affordable option. Their sound output manages to sound rich and textured, and of a superior quality, and they make a great entry-level option if you're looking to test the waters with open-back headphones.

However, for our best overall option, we've picked out the Sennheiser HD 660 S. Priced firmly in the mid-range, you'll save a considerable amount of money compared to the Sennheiser HD 800 S, while still enjoying premium sound quality with virtually no distortion. With ultra-fast impulse fidelity across the board, they highlight every tiny nuance in your music, while minimizing intrusion from the outside world. In terms of form, function, and price point, these are the best open-back headphones you can get right now.
