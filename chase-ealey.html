Chase Ealey Bio, Age, Height, Boyfriend, Net Worth, Wiki

March 2024 · 5 minute read

Chase Ealey is an American track and field athlete who focuses on throwing events. She has amazed the world with her incredible strength and is seen in throwing events like Shot- put. It is known that she played for Oklahoma Stet Cowgirls and Club Desert High Performance. She is coached by Paul Wilson. In 2012 she was a New Mexico State Shot Put champion with a throw score of 14.40 meters. Recently she has been seen competing at the 2022 World Championship in Eugene, Oregon, and is the first American woman to win the Shot Put world title by grabbing the Gold as well.

Early Life

Chase Ealey was born on 20th July 1994 in Springfield, Illinois, United States. Michelle Naranjo is her mother however, no information about her father is disclosed in the media. She has an older sister whose name is Taylor Ealey. At a young age, she was very athletic and actively participated in various sports. Chase has completed her high school education at Los Alamos High School which is located in Los Alamos, New Mexico. Further, she decided to pursue a career in the future so, for the same she used to participate in various competitions and went to Oklahoma State University. She was also being trained professionally for the same.

Chase Ealey Career

Chase Ealey started her career as a professional shot-put player. In 2013 she participated in USA Junior Outdoor Track & Field Championship and represented Oklahoma State Cowgirls in Drake Stadium (Drake University), the Big 12 Conference Indoor Track and Field Championship at Iowa State University and Baylor University, and the 2013 NCAA Division I Outdoor Track and Field Championship at University of Oregon. In 2014 and 2015 she played at the Big 12 Conference Outdoor Track and Field Championship at Iowa State University, Big 12 Conference Outdoor Track and Field Championship at Texas Tech University, 2014 NCAA Division I Outdoor Track and Field Championship at the University of Oregon, 2015 NCAA Division I Indoor Track and Field Championship at University of Arkansas.

She participated in the 2016 United States Olympic Trials (Track and Field) at the University of Oregon, Big 12 Conference Indoor Track and Field Championship at Iowa State University, 2016 NCAA Division I Indoor Track and Field Championship at the University of Alabama Birmingham, Big 12 Conference Outdoor Track and Field Championship in Texas Christian University and 2016 NCAA Division I Outdoor Track and Field Championship in University of Oregon. She has represented Nike Inc in a few of her matches as well. The matches were the 2019 USA Outdoor Track and Field Championship, 2020 USA Indoor Track and Field Championship, 2021 United States Olympic Trials, 2022 USATF Indoor Track and Field Championship, and 2022 USA Outdoor Track and Field Championship.

Rise to Fame

Along with that, she competed at the 2017 USA Indoor Track and Field Championship, 2017 USA Outdoor Track and Field Championship, 2019 USA Indoor Track and Field Championship, 2018 USA Outdoor Track and Field championship, and 2019 USA Indoor Track and Field Championship. Currently she is participating at World Indoor Championship at Belgrade, Silver, and won a Silver with a score of 20.21m. She was also seen at World Outdoor Championship and won a gold medal with a score of 20.49m. It is known that she tied Michelle Carter’s record of North Americana and National in the championship shotput final.
