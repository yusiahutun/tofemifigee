Early childhood 

The personality was born in Creswell, Oregon, on 27th December 1962 and was raised Christian, with his father being a Presbyterian pastor. He attended Creswell High School and was actively involved in sports, playing as a point guard for the basketball team.

Did Mark Few play college basketball? No. He graduated and transitioned to join Linfield College but could not play basketball due to a dislocated shoulder. Few later transferred to the University of Oregon, where luck was not on his side.

The personality could also not play baseball as the institution had scrapped its baseball program. Few quit sports and graduated with a BS in physical education from the University of Oregon.

Career

When did Mark Few start coaching? He started coaching as an amateur, unpaid part-time assistant at Creswell High School in 1983. He subsequently advanced his ranks over the years to a paid position working at Oregon summer basketball camps.

He landed another job as an assistant coach at Sheldon High School in Eugene. In 1989, Mark began professional coaching when he served as coaching staff for Gonzaga University under the then-coach Dan Fitzgerald.

Mark has become one of the most successful head coaches in NCAA history since assuming the Bulldogs helm in 1999. He has been in charge of Gonzaga University for more than 24 seasons, and his excellent coaching tactics made the Bulldog Zags rise from obscurity to major title contenders.

The Bulldog's impressive streak has seen them qualify for 23 straight consecutive NCAA Tournaments since 1999, becoming a household name nationwide. They share the accolade as jointly third with Kansas and Michigan State in active qualification streaks.

The Bulldog Zags had only failed to feature in the NCAA in 2019-20 when the tournament was cancelled owing to the COVID-19 pandemic, where the team had secured an automatic bid.

On the international stage, the Gonzaga coach has also been an integral part of Team US, participating in the recently concluded FIBA 2023 World Cup. He has also been named on the 2024 Olympics coaching staff, along with other A-list names such as Steve Kerr and Tyronn Lue.

Mark Few's accolades include being named the West Coast Conference Coach of the Year for six consecutive seasons between 2001 and 2006. He has also been the recipient of the National Coach of the Year in 2016–17, as per the Associated Press, and the USBWA Henry Iba Award.

Wife and children 

The Gonzaga coach married Marcy Laca in 1994, and the couple has four children together. Does Mark Few have a son? Yes. He has three sons, namely Joseph Dillon James, Colt Walker Norman, and Austin James and one daughter, Julia Ann Elizabeth.

Mark Few's family live with him in Spokane, Washington and have also been very supportive of his career, as seen by their frequent attendance in matches.

Few is a philanthropist and, together with his wife Marcy, has organised an annual charity golf tournament under the Coaches vs. Cancer umbrella, raising over $1 million for the American Cancer Society since 2002. 

Situated in the Spokane region, the non-profit organisation has helped finance cancer screening while also devoting more resources to cancer patients and their families to finance medical treatment causes and has raised the Community Cancer Fund to well over $25 million.
