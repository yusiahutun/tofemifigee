She's not exactly known for keeping things PG when it comes to her ensembles.

And last week as she jetted to a secret luxurious location to shoot a music video with rapper French Montana, Nicki Minaj treated her Twitter followers to a few racy snaps.

Showing off her voluptuous figure, the Super Bass chart-topper, 30, gifted her fans with a view of her derriere as she impersonated a Buddha statue last Thursday.

Cheeky: Nicki Minaj gave her unique impression of the Buddha pose as she showed off her derriere in a neon green bikini in an image she posted to Twitter

With roses and chocolates clearly out this year, Nicki offered her fans a Valentine's Day gift in the form of three risque shots.

Accompanying the images with a caption full of hashtags, she tweeted: 'Happy #VagDay boyz... #JerkResponsibly #prettygangmakenoise #freaksoniTunes'.

Dressed in a neon bright green triangle two-piece, Minaj sports a white sunhat as her black locks rest on her shoulders.

Taking to the poolside in a pair of white gladiator stilettos, Nicki gets into a squatted position again as she looks into the distance of her picturesque surroundings.

Daring: In another shot Nicki squats on the ledge of the pool in a pair of heels as she shows off her picturesque view

Taking a break from American Idol, where she is a judge alongside Randy Jackson, Keith Urban and Mariah Carey, Nicki is currently in paradise filming scenes for French Montana's third single.

Minaj offers a guest verse to on the track entitled Freaks, which is taken from the Bad Boy rapper's debut album studio album Excuse My French.

On Tuesday, Montana posted a picture of himself and Nicki on the set of the video, which looks like it will feature cameos from Tyga and Wiz Khalifa.

Not very PG: The 30-year-old has proved that she isn't shy of showing off her curves

On set: Nicki is currently gearing up to release a single alongside rapper French Montana, entitled Freaks

Away from the music side of her career, Nicki became the latest star to be immortalized as a character in a comic book.

In a new comic book, entitled Fame: Nicki Minaj, the rapper plays herself but as a superhero who uses her powers to rise from the underground hip hop scene to become a mainstream star.

Written by Michael Troy and drawn by Jill Lamarina, Nicki joins the likes of Lady Gaga, Katy Perry and Nick Jonas, who have all featured in the Bluewaters Comics series Fame.
