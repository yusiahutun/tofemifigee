The first novel ever written is "The Tale of Genji."

Believed to have been completed in the early 11th century by Japanese noblewoman Murasaki Shikibu, "The Tale of Genji" is regarded as the world's first novel. Set in the imperial court of Heian-period Japan, this literary masterpiece offers a captivating portrayal of courtly life, romance, and societal customs.

The longest novel ever written is "Artamène."

With a staggering 13,095 pages, "Artamène" holds the record for the longest novel ever written. Penned by French author Madeleine de Scudéry, this epic work of fiction was published in ten volumes between 1649 and Its immense length and intricate plot make it a monumental achievement in the realm of literature.

The most expensive book ever purchased is the Codex Leicester by Leonardo da Vinci.

In 1994, Bill Gates acquired the Codex Leicester for a jaw-dropping $30.8 million. This remarkable manuscript, written by the legendary polymath Leonardo da Vinci, contains his scientific and artistic musings, offering unparalleled insights into his visionary mind.

The first book printed in English is "The Recuyell of the Historyes of Troye."

Published by William Caxton in 1473, "The Recuyell of the Historyes of Troye" marked the advent of English printing. Caxton's pioneering efforts played a pivotal role in popularizing the English language and facilitating the dissemination of knowledge through printed materials.

The best-selling book of all time is the Bible.

With an estimated 5 billion copies sold, the Bible stands as the best-selling book in history. Its profound impact on literature, religion, and culture has solidified its status as a timeless and influential work.

The first book ever printed is the Gutenberg Bible.

Crafted by Johannes Gutenberg in the 1450s, the Gutenberg Bible revolutionized the world of publishing. This landmark achievement in printing technology paved the way for the dissemination of knowledge on a mass scale.

The most translated book is "The Little Prince" by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry.

Originally published in French, "The Little Prince" has been translated into over 300 languages and dialects. Its universal themes and enchanting narrative have captivated readers worldwide, earning it the title of the most translated book in history.

The largest book in the world is "Bhutan: A Visual Odyssey Across the Last Himalayan Kingdom."

Measuring a remarkable 5 by 7 feet when open, "Bhutan: A Visual Odyssey Across the Last Himalayan Kingdom" holds the distinction of being the largest book ever published. Its breathtaking imagery and extensive content make it a truly awe-inspiring literary marvel.

The first book written using a typewriter is "Life on the Mississippi" by Mark Twain.

Mark Twain's "Life on the Mississippi" was the first book to be submitted to a publisher in typewritten form. This marked a significant milestone in the history of literature and technology, showcasing the evolving methods of book production.

The most prolific author is L. Ron Hubbard.

Renowned for his extensive literary output, L. Ron Hubbard authored a staggering 1,084 works, spanning various genres and subjects. His remarkable productivity and diverse contributions have solidified his legacy as one of the most prolific writers in history.

The first novel written on a typewriter is "The Adventures of Tom Sawyer" by Mark Twain.

Mark Twain's classic novel "The Adventures of Tom Sawyer" holds the distinction of being the first novel to be completed on a typewriter. This technological innovation in the realm of writing marked a significant shift in the way authors produced their literary works.

The most expensive book ever sold at auction is "The Bay Psalm Book."

In 2013, "The Bay Psalm Book" fetched a staggering $14.2 million at auction, making it the most expensive book ever sold. This historic volume, printed in 1640, holds immense significance as the first book printed in British North America.

The first book to be adapted into a feature film is "Les Misérables" by Victor Hugo.

Victor Hugo's monumental novel "Les Misérables" was the first book to be adapted into a feature film, marking a significant milestone in the intersection of literature and cinema. Its enduring narrative has continued to inspire numerous adaptations across various artistic mediums.

The first novel to be published on the internet is "Soul Mate 2.0" by Brad Magnarella.

In 2009, "Soul Mate 2.0" made history as the first full-length novel to be published exclusively on the internet. This pioneering digital release signaled a new era in literary distribution, embracing the digital landscape as a platform for storytelling.

The longest sentence ever printed is 823 words long.

Found in "Les Misérables" by Victor Hugo, the longest sentence ever printed spans a remarkable 823 words. This astonishing literary feat showcases Hugo's unparalleled mastery of language and storytelling, cementing his place as a literary titan.
