Adele tells crowd at Las Vegas residency about her 'baptism' while swimming on holiday in Jamaica

September 2024 · 4 minute read

Adele has revealed that she had a 'baptism' while on holiday in Jamaica and added she 'truly believes' God was in the the ocean.

The singer has been performing week-in, week-out at her Las Vegas residency in Ceasar's Palace, and in-between belting out her biggest hits she has told them anecdotes to bring her fans closer.

And over the weekend, she revealed her spiritual awakening while spending some time in the Caribbean.

Revealing: Adele has revealed that she had a 'baptism' while on holiday in Jamaica and added she 'truly believes' God was in the the ocean

According to The Star, Adele told her fans she was swimming in the Jamaican resort GoldenEye Bay, where the James Bond creator Ian Fleming wrote numerous of the 007 stories.

'This might sound weird. But I had a baptism in Jamaica in the ocean,' she said.

'I gave it to myself. It wasn’t not like official. I went into Goldeneye and I went into that water and I came out a completely different person there.

'I truly believe that God was there in that ocean.'

She confessed that she was 'off her face' but did not explain why she was so inebriated.  

Epiphany: The Oh My God hitmaker has been performing week-in, week-out at her Las Vegas residency in Ceasar's Palace, and in-between belting out her biggest hits she has told them anecdotes to bring her fans closer

The Oh My God hitmaker began telling the story after seeing a particular group of guests in the audience of her Stateside show.

Model Amira Philipps had been one of the group to have flown in from the Caibbean to Vegas especially for her concert.

During her show, she also referenced criticism she received on social media back in 2020.

She was accused of 'cultural appropriation' for wearing a Jamaican-flag bikini and Bantu knots to mark Notting Hill Carnival.

The singer, 34, said she 'didn't read the f***ing room' with her post but that she didn't take the picture down as that would have meant she was 'acting like it never happened'.

Reflecting: Adele also made reference to the accusations of 'cultural appropriation' for wearing a Jamaican-flag bikini and bantu knots to mark Notting Hill Carnival in summer 2020

In a picture shared to her Instagram, the songstress, posing in the garden of her $9.5million Beverly Hills home, wore her hair in Bantu knots leading some to slam the choice as 'insensitive'.

Speaking over the weekend, she seemed to make light of the situation: 'I don’t know if we should bring that up. The Americans didn’t like it, but they (The Jamaicans) loved it.

'Can you imagine if you came dressed as me when I was in Jamaica?'

It comes after Adele reacted to a video of a fan reportedly trying to woo her with his phone number during the third weekend of her Las Vegas residency on Sunday. 

Funny: Adele, 34, has reacted to a video of a fan reportedly trying to woo her with his phone number during the third weekend of her Las Vegas residency on Sunday

The songstress, 34, cleared up the rumours during her performance as she insisted that the concert goer in question was actually 'trying to sell her a car.'

Despite explaining the funny exchange, she later joked that her boyfriend Rich Paul was 'livid'.

In a clip from the weekend, a man can be seen trying to pass Adele a note as she made her way through the crowd, with the singer giggling and handing it back. 

The following day during her show at Caesars Palace, she cleared up the gossip and explained: 'Someone texted me something this morning saying that a guy tried to give me his number last night when I was walking through this way. 

'He wasn’t, he was just trying to make me buy a car from him, [which] was actually what he was doing,' she laughed, in the clip shared by concert goer Jorge Preciado.
