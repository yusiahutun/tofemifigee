The 10 best Canadian hockey players of all time as of 2023

September 2024 · 7 minute read

Hockey is among the most loved sports in the Great White North. It's more of a culture than a sport to them, and that's why most of the greatest NHL players are Canadian. The best Canadian hockey players are remembered for their impacts on the field and incredible stats, which include goals, assists, and wins.

Since the formation of the National Hockey League (NHL) in 1917, fans have witnessed many talents come and go. There have been many incredible hockey players in the past who are still remembered to date. There are also tons of great hockey players in the NHL today who will be recognized in the same realm.

NFL features players from all over the world, including the USA and European countries. However, Canada leads the charge in terms of NHL legends.

1. Wayne Gretzky

Wayne Gretzky (born on 26th January 1961) is a former professional hockey player and head coach. He played 20 seasons in NHL (1979 – 1999) and featured for four teams; Edmonton Oilers, New York Rangers, St Louis Blues, and Los Angeles Kings.

Wayne was nicknamed the "Great One" and has been called the greatest hockey player by ex-players, coaches, managers, and sportswriters.

Why is Wayne Gretzky the greatest Canadian? To date, Gretzky is the only player to have recorded 200 points in a season – amazingly, he did it four times. He also holds a fantastic record for most goals in a season, with 92. Single-season records aside – Gretzky has a total of 894 career goals and 1963 assists which are just massive.

2. Mario Lemieux

Some fans argue that Mario is the best Canadian hockey player of all time. They believe that Lemieux's overall totals would be greater than Wayne Gretzky's if not for his back injuries and being diagnosed with Hodgkin's disease while in his prime.

3. Bobby Orr

In today's NHL, several defensemen have incredibly high points - thanks to Bobby Orr. Before Orr, the primary role of a defenseman was to keep the puck out of the net; they were not an offensive threat. But he changed this narrative by finishing his career with a record 100 points on different occasions.

The stats prove Bobby's dominance in NHL. His 1,333 points per game is 15th all-time in league history. If not for his recurring knee injuries, Orr would undoubtedly be the highest all-time scoring defenseman. He managed to record 915 points and 270 goals in just 657 games.

4. Gordie Howe

Gordie isn’t the best but probably the most famous Canadian hockey player. Fans refer to him as Mr. Hockey because of his impact on the field. Before Wayne Gretzky and Bobby Orr, he was the best.

It's not the stats that made Howe special but his longevity in the game. He made 1,767 career appearances in NHL and 419 in WHA (World Hockey Association). The other Howe spectacular achievement that's remembered to date is the "Gordie Howe Hat Trick." A hat trick is accomplished when a player gets a fight, an assist, and, most importantly, a goal.

When it comes to stats, Howe was also a beast. He got a total of 1,850 points which ranks fourth. He also has four NFL cups and six Art Rose and Harts Trophies on his sleeves.

5. Mark Messier

In the 1980s, the Edmonton Oilers were invisible. Mark Messier featured for Edmonton Oilers in this era and played alongside the best hockey player of all time, Wayne Gretzky. Throughout his career, Mark won six Stanley Cups; five with the Oilers and one with New York Rangers. But these are just a few of the trophies he won.

6. Steve Yzerman

Steve played for Detroit Red Wings (1983 -2006) and is remembered for his extraordinary leadership. He was named the captain of Redwing in 1986 and made over 1,300 games with the armband. Under his captaincy, Detroit Red Wings won five President's Trophies and three Stanley Cups.

He was also the super captain of the Canadian national Hockey team that won gold in the 1984 Canada Cup and the 2002 and 2005 Olympics. His stats were also attractive; he got 185 points in playoff games and 1,755 in 1,555 NHL appearances.

7. Jean Arthur Beliveau

Like others on this list, Jean Beliveau is regarded as one of the best Canadian hockey players because of his incredible stats. He had a prolific career with the famous Montreal Canadiens (1953 -1971).

Jean was named the team captain in 1961 and scored 507 goals, which is impressive. He also won many cups/ trophies, including ten Stanley Cups, Hart Trophy, Conn Smythe Trophy, and more.

After his professional career, Jean was appointed as the Canadiens' vice president. Later, in 1972 he got an induction into the coveted Hockey Hall of Fame – just one year after his retirement. Usually, players have to wait for three years to earn induction into the special Hockey Hall of Fame.

8. Maurice Richard

Maurice Richard was undoubtedly ahead of the game and is one of the best Canadian NHL players of all time. At his retirement, he had set 17 league records.

9. Bobby Hull

Bobby Hull was a key player for NHL Chicago Black Hawks (1957 – 1972). He was one of the fastest players of his time, earning him the nickname "The Golden Jets." In the 1959 -1960 season, Bobby scored 39 goals. Two years later, he equaled Maurice Richard's record of 50 goals. In 1965 – 1966, he got better stats of 54 goals and 43 assists.

Bobby's incredible performances helped his team, the Black Hawks, win the Stanley Cup. He also won other prestigious trophies, including the Hart Trophy, Lady Byng Trophy, and Ross Trophy.

10. Sydney Crosby

Sydney Crosby joined Pittsburgh Penguins in 2005 while 18 years and won the 2005 World Junior Championship. In 2007 he won the Hart Trophy and Art Ross Trophy and became the league's youngest captain. Crosby was the youngest league player in his first NHL season to reach 100 points.

That's not all; he continued producing incredible performances. In 2009, he won the prestigious Stanley Cup, and then the Rocket Richard Trophy in 2010. Sydney's fame soared in the 2010 Winter Olympics in Vancouver when he scored a dramatic goal that saw Canada win the gold medal.
