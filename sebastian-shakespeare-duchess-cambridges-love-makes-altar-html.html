When the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge married, Kate’s only male friend from her schooldays invited to the Westminster Abbey ceremony was Willem Marx.

This weekend, Marx, who was the Duchess’s first love at £32,280-a-year Marlborough College, tied the knot himself with another striking brunette.

Kate, however, was conspicuous by her absence at the nuptials on the Amalfi Coast in Italy. ‘It was a joyful occasion,’ says a member of the congregation in the Catholic Basilica in Castellabate. ‘It’s a shame Kate couldn’t make it.’

Old flame: The Duchess of Cambridge's 'first boyfriend' Willem Marx married his partner Johanna Botta on the Amalfi Coast in Italy with friends from the US and the UK

The bride, Johanna Botta, is an Italian TV presenter who works in America, where Oxford graduate Marx has forged a career as a journalist.

After William and Kate briefly split up in 2007, Marx was pictured leaving Boujis nightclub in South Kensington with the future Queen.

Before Johanna, he courted brunette Lancome model Elettra Rossellini Wiedemann, granddaughter of Casablanca star Ingrid Bergman.

After spending time in war-torn Iraq for American PR firm the Lincoln Group, he boasted in a magazine article that when he explained to one ‘raven-haired beauty’ in a restaurant in Edinburgh the importance of his trip to Baghdad, she whispered: ‘That’s very sexy.’

From Homeland to Throneland. Old Etonian actor Damian Lewis has clearly been enjoying playing Henry VIII in the BBC adaptation of Hilary Mantel’s novel Wolf Hall.

‘It’s absolutely fantastic playing a genocidal maniac,’ Lewis told the Chalke Valley History Festival.

 

‘Hilary Mantel’s Henry is a complex, tender, brilliant, athletic, poetic dreamer.

'It’s been fun playing the pre-1536 Henry — a more svelte, 34-inch-waisted king.

'After 1536, in the last ten years of his life, he ballooned, rather like Elvis.’

Bonnie Greer: Blue plaque panel a whitewash

Bonnie Greer OBE has resigned from the English Heritage panel that decides who is  entitled to one of its coveted Blue Plaques.

This follows the departure of two fellow panellists who were outraged that a slew of celebrities had been asked to join them — including Big Brother creator Sir Peter Bazalgette, the scientists/TV presenters Lord Winston and Brian Cox, banker Rupert Hambro and FA chairman Greg Dyke.

Broadcaster Greer tells me: ‘Making the committee more celebrity-drenched, I suppose, makes sense in a climate in which the arts and culture are becoming more beholden to big ticket benefactors.

‘But the panel is now both hideously white and hideously male and I refuse to be the token black woman to enable them to claim “diversity”.’

Actress and wildlife campaigner Anna Friel, 37, was in celebratory mood at a party thrown by Evening Standard owner Evgeny Lebedev at the weekend.

She had just learned  that a British oil and gas company, which had plans to explore in Africa’s oldest national park, has decided to pull out of the venture. 

Friel — who has been dating unkempt Welsh actor Rhys Ifans since 2011 — had been campaigning against the plans, which threatened the habitat of gorillas, on behalf of wildlife charity WWF.
