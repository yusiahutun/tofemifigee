Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen beauty interview

February 2024 · 3 minute read

You've probably already heard of Ashley Olsen and Mary-Kate Olsen's fragrance line because, like everything they turn their hand to, it has a cult following.

Embodying the design duo's innate style – timeless elegance with a modern edge – the Nirvana perfumes from their brand Elizabeth and James just launched in the UK with two worldwide exclusives at Harrods (and a stampede).

Elizabeth and James Nirvana Amethyst and Nirvana French Grey

To celebrate the news, we spoke exclusively to fashion's hardest working partnership about the move into fragrance and their unique approach to the business of beauty.

"For each Nirvana fragrance, we challenged the perfumers to keep the scent simple and true to the raw notes," Ashley tells us, explaining that their intrinsically understated style totally translates into scent – and not just in the application. (Although Ashley does state "less is more" as her top spritzing tip.)

"Ashley and I are drawn to scents that are pure," Mary-Kate explains. "Single notes can be very nostalgic." Depending on her mood she rotates her fragrances, while Ashley is loyal to wearing "pure rose and sandalwood," which sounds typically chic.

The Nirvana fragrances, of which there are now six in total, boast the same packaging (textured glass bottles with architectural lines) in different colours – designed, of course, by the sisters themselves.

"The bottle's texture is inspired by a vintage object," Mary-Kate tells us. "We wanted something that would remain beautiful on its own." Which it does. Like most fashion house fragrances this is our affordable, entry-point slice of the brand's style – something so many of us want to emulate in the Olsen's case. And, as is the democratic "beauty of fragrance", Ashley says they can imagine "every person" wearing them.

Come September the UK line-up will see the new Nirvana Amethyst and Nirvana French Grey fragrances stand alongside the original Nirvana White and Nirvana Black scents, plus Nirvana Rose and Nirvana Bourbon, which are already hits in the States.

"We're excited to be introducing Elizabeth and James Nirvana to a global audience and new international markets," Ashley says.

The 31-year-old explains that one of the most noticeable differences between the beauty and fashion worlds is the pace. "Lead times are longer in beauty because the product is not seasonal like it is in fashion," she says, reminding us that these are not one-season wonders, but possible contenders for your future signature scent.

Getty

We're hoping the launch of their fragrances here will pave the way for their coveted Perfume Candles, and award-winning Nirvana Black and White Dry Shampoos. Plus more?

"We grow our brands organically – taking the time to find the right opportunities and identify niches in the market," Mary-Kate explains. "There is a lot of opportunity in colour [cosmetics] and hair that we are excited to explore further when the time is right."

No doubt it will be worth the wait.
