Oasis reunion planning begins as agent plots comeback shows at iconic venue

February 2024 · 7 minute read

SPECULATION about an Oasis reunion has reached new heights as brothers Liam and Noel Gallagher have both begun to flirt with the idea, sending fans into a frenzy.

I am told a serious play for a massive four-night set of concerts at Knebworth, Herts, in 2025 is being made by Liam’s live agents Wasserman.

It would be the first time the band have played together since performing at V Festival in August 2009.

The Manchester rockers played two record-breaking shows in the grounds of stately home Knebworth in 1996, performing in front of 250,000 people in total.

One-time rival Robbie Williams then broke that record in 2003 when he played to 375,000 people over three nights at the Hertfordshire estate.

The venue has a capacity of around 125,000 people.

“It would be the perfect way for Oasis to come back together again and wouldn’t involve a tiresome touring schedule.”

But if all members can agree to the terms, 2025 would work for both of them.

This month guitarist Bonehead revealed that he wanted the band to reunite in 2024 to celebrate the 30th anniversary of their debut album Definitely Maybe

Noel also seemed more open to the idea.

He admitted he is getting along “brilliantly” with his sibling at the moment and can no longer rule it out.

He said: “You should never say never, but it would have to take an extraordinary set of circumstances.

1D? Get ready for 3D

THERE has been a mini One Direction reunion – just over seven years after the boys put the band on ice.

I can reveal that Liam Payne headed to the studio with his former 1D bandmates Niall Horan and Louis Tomlinson in Los Angeles, where he has been working on his eagerly awaited second album.

I am told the trio recorded a track for Liam’s record, with guest vocals from Niall and Louis.

An insider said: “Liam is great pals with Niall and Louis, so loved catching up with them while they were all in LA. Being in the studio together felt natural, so they all jumped on a track.

“It was second nature to them singing together and felt like old times. They had a right laugh.

“Producers are hoping Liam includes it on his finished album, fans will love it.”

Caught live: Lewis Capaldi

LEWIS Capaldi took to the stage for a Q&A night to celebrate the release of his new album Broken By Desire To Be Heavenly Sent – and revealed how problematic it is making music these days.

After singing tracks including Someone You Loved and Wish You The Best, he said: “The hardest song I wrote on the album was Pointless. I got this really good idea for a verse. Then I wrote the chorus and the middle eight and I sent it to someone and I said, ‘Do you think we will get sued?’. They were like, ‘Yes. Do not release it’. So I had to change it.”

He told Friday’s crowd at Pryzm nightclub in South West London: “Luckily I wrote the song with Ed Sheeran, Johnny McDaid and Steve Mac and they have great lawyers.”

Group chat

Liam, who is looking better than ever with his new sober lifestyle, sent One Direction fans into a frenzy last week when he revealed the band had set up a new group chat.

Asked during last week’s KSI and Joe Fournier boxing match at the OVO Arena in Wembley, North West London, about a reunion, fight fan Liam said: “I don’t know — but we just started a new group chat, so that is something” The interviewer told the camera, “1D is back soon”, after which Liam simply shrugged as he kept tight-lipped about any comeback.

Kamille's Nile high

BRIT Award-winner Kamille has revealed Nile Rodgers gave her and Beyonce the same advice about following their own instincts.

She was dubbed the fifth member of Little Mix after writing Black Magic and Shout Out To My Ex, and scooped the Outstanding Song Collection gong at the Ivor Novello Awards on Thursday.

Kamille worked with CHIC legend Nile on her track Muscle Memory at Abbey Road Studios and explained: “He said to me what he said to Beyonce when they were working on Cuff – ‘You just need to do what feels right for you and you need to ignore everybody else. If this is music that comes from your heart, you need to put it out and that is all that should matter’.”

She added: “He took it to the next level and made it a Nile Rodgers-sounding record, which for me is just such an honour.”
