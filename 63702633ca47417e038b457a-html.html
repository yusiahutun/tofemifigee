&quot;Selling Tampa&quot; got canceled and cast members believe it's racist, per report

August 2024 Â· 2 minute read

"Selling Tampa" will not have a second season after it wasn't renewed and among the cast members there's some that think that racial discrimination is involved in this decision, according to a report by Page Six.

Juawana Colbert, member of the cast said that she believes that when they show black women in a different light, like bickering, fighting and name-calling, shows get more than one season, and she believes that's not what they're displaying.

"I feel like we weren't given a second chance, possibly because of what we represented as minority women."

Netflix is cancelling "Selling Tampa"

Netflix debuted "Selling Tampa" on December 2021, with a cast almost entirely of women of color, with Juawana Colbert, Colony Reeves, Anne-Sophie Petit, Tennille Moore, Karla Giorgo, Rena Frazier and Alexis Williams.

The series shows Sharelle Rosado and her real estate agency Allure Realty, around which the show is centered.

Cast member are reportedly out of words due to this situation, with allegedly hundreds of fans messaging them daily to ask about a new season.

Adam DiVello gave them the bad news

Adam DiVello, the creator of the "Selling" franchise asked them to join him and producer Skyler Wakil in a Zoom call, where they were told that the show wasn't continuing.

"Adam specifically said [the decision] was related to numbers but never gave any additional information. I don't know if he was talking about rating numbers or budget numbers. He just said numbers." Colbert added.
