She added, “I’m kinda disappointed, I wanted it to be somebody famous, then there would be more to talk about…”

While Jim and Deelishis co-parent their only child, it’s worth noting that the pair were never married. Jim and Deelishis’ daughter, Jasmine Davis, was 22 years old as of 2022. She was a freshman at Spelman College circa May 2018.

Jim Colmer, Deelishis alleged baby father, is happily married to his wife, Donna Clarette Comer for over 50 years now. Reportedly, the lovely duo got married in the year 1969. On August 23, 2015, the couple celebrated their 4th wedding anniversary.

On the auspicious occasion, Donna shared a picture with her husband on her Facebook and wrote, “Today Jim (Earl) and I are celebrating our 46th wedding anniversary. No special plans, just thankful for God’s grace that we are here to be together. We are truly blessed.”

Talking about Jim’s wife, Donna Clarette Comer, she received her education from Oakland Community College. She was in a former assembly line at General Motors from 1971 to 1978.

Jim and Donna have a son, whose name is Jimmie Comer II. According to Jimmie’s Facebook, he works at Comer Produce LLC and Intergalactic LLC.

Furthermore, Jim and Donna are grandparents to Justin, Jalen, and Jasmine Comer.

Jim Colmer Age

Jim Colmer was born in the year 1947, which means his age was 74 as of March 2022. He celebrates his birthday on the 6th of January every year.

Jim Colmer Net Worth

As of this writing, Deelishis’s baby father Jim Colmer’s net worth was reported to be above $15 million.

Jim attended Wayne State University for his undergraduate studies.

Talking about his career, Jim has over three decades of experience working in leadership roles with different Fortune 500 companies. He is the owner and founder of Comer Holdings, LLC, which is located in Southfield, Michigan.

As per its website, Comer Foundation “is committed to the improvement of the Detroit area and surrounding communities by providing resources which support educational, economic and social development. Through charitable giving and community partnerships, Comer Foundation encourages personal and professional development, initiated during early childhood.”

Before working at Comer Holdings, Jim worked at Lear Corporation as a President of the North American Joint Ventures and Diversity. As a President of the North American Joint Ventures and Diversity of Lear Corporation, he was responsible for the growth and development of Lear’s minority joint ventures and supplier diversity activities in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico. He also oversaw the company’s diversity-related initiatives for employees, as well as Lear’s support to the community.

Jim has also worked at the Mazda Corporation as the quality manager. He held several quality and manufacturing positions including Superintendent of Quality/Inspection for The General Motors Corporation.

In 2003, Jim was appointed as the Commissioner of The Michigan Community Services Commission by Michigan governor Jennifer Granholm. Later, in 2005, Jim was also honored with President’s Volunteer Service Award by President George W. Bush.
