Kate Moss shows off a frightening accessory in friend's wedding photos

February 2024 · 3 minute read

Kate Moss attended the wedding of her close friend beauty journalist and former Vogue editor Tish Weinstock, sporting a very unlikely accessory. 

Guests including Kate her daughter Lila Moss, Adwoa Aboah and Princess Olympia of Greece attended the three-day event which took place over Halloween weekend at Belvoir Castle, (the 11th-Century ancestral home of the Duke of Rutland and his family.) "I thought, what's scarier than a wedding? … A Halloween wedding," Tish explained to British Vogue about her decision to host a spooky season themed soiree, "October in England just felt right," explained the bride, "The castle is so beautiful and October is my birthday month."

Guests including Kate were invited to don fancy dress for Friday night's pre-wedding pub quiz on the Belvoir estate hosted by Jack Guinness, with the bride and groom dressing up as Baz Luhrmann's Romeo and Juliet, a fashion-set approved costume that Nicola Peltz and Brooklyn Beckham also wore this Halloween.

Kate took to the themed weekend with gusto, donning realistic looking fangs for her Vampire Bride costume she wore to the costume party, in a belted white lace high-necked dress and a fluffy cloak. She sported a dark wig and channelled her inner vampiric bride, complete with a billowing mesh veil, while the dress code for the ceremony the next day was "black tie gothic".

A few days ago the pair attended Diet Coke's 40th birthday together

Earlier this week attended Diet Coke's 40th birthday arm in arm with Tish. Ever the fashion maverick, Kate championed the 'visible lingerie' trend which has taken the sartorial set by storm this season. Unlike the naked dress, where the wearer wears flesh coloured thongs to not ruin the 'naked' illusion of the sheer gown, visible lingerie means that the undergarments are worn proudly on show under translucent materials. 

The 48-year-old supermodel made sure all eyes were on her in the plunging cowl neck gown, which was adorned with a shimmering embellished aztec pattern, set atop sheer black gauzy material. Underneath she wore a black pair of briefs which only highlighted the transparency of the dress.
