Is Joe Lombardi Fired? Career And Net Worth

Joseph Philip Lombardi, a former player, and current American football coach, serves as the offensive coordinator for the Los Angeles Chargers of the National Football League.

He managed the offense for the Detroit Lions during 2014 and 2015 and from 2016 and 2020, he coached the quarterbacks for the New Orleans Saints.

Lombardi has served as the Atlanta Falcons’ assistant coach in the past. Coach Vince Lombardi, who is in the Pro Football Hall of Fame, is his grandpa.

In his debut season, Lombardi guided the Chargers to the top passing offense in the AFC and the fourth-best overall offense in the NFL (282.4 yards per game).

Lombardi’s offense consisted of four Pro Bowl players: Keenan Allen, Justin Herbert, Corey Linsley, and Rashawn Slater, all of whom were anticipated to start for the AFC.

Lombardi was hired by the Detroit Lions in 2014 to be Jim Caldwell’s offensive coordinator. On October 26, 2015, the Lions fired him along with a number of other coaching staff members after a 1-6 start to the season.

On January 25, 2021, Lombardi was hired by the Los Angeles Chargers to serve as offensive coordinator for head coach Brandon Staley.
