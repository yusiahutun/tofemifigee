One piece, three ways: the sleeveless coat

April 2024 · 2 minute read

Iris & Ink/The Outnet

The most successful pieces in any woman's wardrobe are those that are timeless, versatile and flattering; investment buys that you can always rely on in times of sartorial need. The sleeveless coat - or longline gilet, if you prefer - is one such piece. While at first glance, it might seem tricky to pull off, it's actually surprisingly simple to style and can instantly elevate your casual T-shirt and jeans or pull together an otherwise unfinished outfit. 

Not convinced? Allow us to demonstrate. Designed exclusively by The Outnet, Iris & Ink's sleeveless coat is perfect for work, evening or weekend. Here's how...

1. For work

Any work capsule wardrobe should contain a well fitting pair of tailored trousers and a classic, crisp white shirt, but adding a sleeveless coat will suddenly transform your look into something far chicer and softer. Finish with a midi heel (something you can walk in all day, preferably), and a roomy tote; this Tory Burch bag is top of our wish list.

Wool gilet, £295, Iris & Ink; linen shirt, £58.50, Rag & Bone; Cotton trousers, £99.45, Joseph; Leather tote, £230, Tory Burch; Leather pumps, £227.50, Alexander Wang; all The Outnet

2. For weekend

Whether brunching with friends, running errands or visiting family, adding a sleeveless coat over your staple T-shirt and jeans will suddenly make you feel that little bit more pulled together. It works just as well with flat trainers as it does with heels, plus add a backpack for a final casual - but not scruffy - touch. 

Wool gilet, £295, Iris & Ink; Linen-jersey t-shirt, £55.20, Theory; Denim jeans, £99, Current/Elliott; Leather trainers, £71.40, Ash; Calf hair and leather backpack, £395, Alexander Wang; all The Outnet

3. For evening

While a pink gilet might not be what immediately comes to mind when you think of dinner or cocktails, the addition of a classic black top and ladylike skirt (we love this one from Alexander McQueen) makes it instantly evening-appropriate. Let your statement coat take centre stage though, and team with neutral accessories that won't overpower; a simple clutch, classic black heels and a pair of earrings are all you need.

Wool gilet, £295, Iris & Ink; Satin-scuba top, £156, 3.1 Phillip Lim, Crystal earrings, £52.50, Elizabeth Cole; Suede and leather clutch, £128.25, Halston Heritage; Leather skirt, £1,718, Alexander McQueen; Patent leather pumps, £248.63, Nicholas Kirkwood; all The Outnet
