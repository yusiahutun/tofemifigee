How Lewis Hamilton won over the sporting world, fashion industry and models

May 2024 · 5 minute read

He may have missed out on a Monaco Grand Prix coup, but Lewis Hamilton's career away from the race track is quietly gaining momentum.

The 30-year-old, who, at 23, shot to global fame as the youngest Formula One world champion ever, is carving a name for himself in the fashion and music industry - and Hollywood seems enamored with him.

Sunday's Grand Prix was case in point. The racing supremo was joined by a gaggle of Hollywood's hot young model pack, including Gigi Hadid, Kendall Jenner and Hailey Baldwin. So how did the boy who grew up on a council estate in Stevenage do so good?

Born in Stevenage in 1985, Lewis' talents became apparent the first time he stepped into a go kart on holiday in Spain at the tender age of six.

His father, who spotted his racing skills, nurtured his talent. When he was ten, a young Lewis met Ron Dennis, team principle at McLaren, at an awards ceremony and told him: 'I'm going to race for you one day...I'm going to race for McLaren.'

Fast-forward three years and Dennis had signed him to McLaren's young driver's programme. The rest, as they say, is history. 

Lewis, who was named BBC Sports Personality of the Year in 2014, has more race victories than any other British driver in the history of Formula One. And his sporting prowess scored him one of Tinseltown's most fanciable women.

Pussycat Dolls singer Nicole Scherzinger and Lewis began dating in November 2007 and quickly became one of Hollywood's most talked-about couples.

Known for their tumultuous relationship, the on-again-off-again pair called it quits in February this year. 

Since then, Lewis, who was awarded an MBE by the Queen in the 2009 New Year Honours and has a waxwork in his honour in Madam Tussauds, has been breaking into the Hollywood scene.

He arrived at the Met Gala this month dressed head-to-toe in Topman with Arcadia boss Sir Philip Green. He was joined by IT girls Emily Ratajkowski, Bella Hadid, Hailey Baldwin, Josephine de la Baume, Natasha Poly, Maryna Linchuk and Lily Donaldson, and partied the night away with the new Hollywood brat pack.

Last week, he hobknobbed with A-listers in Cannes and was seated next to newly single Gigi Hadid at the Cinema Against AIDS gala for amfAR at the Hotel du Cap-Eden-Roc, sparking romance rumours. 

Along with Kendall Jenner, the model, fresh from her split from Cody Simpson, put on a cosy display with the racing driver at the Monaco Grand Prix, as he placed his arms tenderly around her.

In a further display of affection, Gigi, 20, posted a snap of her with Nicole Scherzinger's ex to Instagram, in which she accompanied the image with a gushing message.

She wrote: 'We love you @lewishamilton. Everyone knows you da champ. #TEAMLH.' 

MailOnline has contacted a spokesperson for Gigi and Lewis for comment.

Proving how far he's come, on Wednesday, Lewis became the highest paid British sportsman in history by signing a £100million contract to stay at Mercedes for three more years.

The deal was announced on Wednesday in Monaco, the tax haven home of the Formula One world champion and venue for Sunday's Grand Prix.

But Hamilton's £100m from Mercedes – a figure that includes potential bonuses – represents only the beginning of Hamilton's future mega-riches. It is understood he retained the freedom to do personal endorsements with companies in and outside the sport, meaning he could more than double his earnings.

He already has a deal with IWC, the luxury Swiss watchmakers, and stars in extra-curricular advertising work for Mercedes' road car division, with whom he has signed a separate extension.

Taken on its own, his Mercedes salary beats Gareth Bale's £23m wages at Real Madrid and dwarfs the £16m in winnings and endorsements earned by golf superstar Rory McIlroy last year.
