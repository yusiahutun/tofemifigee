The talented star portrays the protagonist Gia - who is a fictional representation of book author Taddeo - as she travels across the US in order to hear the stories of three women.

Those characters include Lina (Betty Gilpin)  who is a housewife whose husband won't kiss her, Sloane (DeWanda Wise) who is an entrepreneur in an open relationship with her husband, and Maggie (Gabrielle Creevy) who plays a student who realizes that she had been preyed upon by her high school teacher.

Shailene revealed that the show is not a typical take on the 'strong women' trope and is more grounded in reality.

She explained: 'Sometimes it’s frustrating, because a lot of the things that I read are all about women’s empowerment and feminism, but the way the stories are being told are actually not portraying women in an empowered way. To me, empowerment simply means truth, vulnerability, acknowledging the good and the bad, the pros, the cons, the ugly, the beauty.

'The conversation I had with Lisa was about that; we’re not here trying to make a show that’s championing women all around the world to become the best versions of themselves – it’s a show that’s just acknowledging the reality that women face.'

 Three Women is set for release sometime in 2023 on Showtime.
