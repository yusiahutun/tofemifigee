Biography TimeLine

Will Ferrell is an American actor, comedian, producer, and writer who has made a name for himself through his work on Saturday Night Live and in a wide range of popular films and TV shows. Here is a detailed biography timeline of Will Ferrell’s life:

  • 1967: Will Ferrell was born on July 16, in Irvine, California.
  • 1986: Ferrell graduated from University High School in Irvine and attended the University of Southern California, where he studied sports broadcasting.
  • 1994: Ferrell joined the cast of the improvisational comedy group The Groundlings, where he honed his skills as a performer and writer.
  • 1995: Ferrell was hired as a writer and cast member on Saturday Night Live, where he gained a following for his memorable characters and comedic performances.
  • 2001: Ferrell left Saturday Night Live to pursue a career in film and television. He appeared in several popular comedies, including “Zoolander,” “Elf,” and “Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy.”
  • 2007: Ferrell starred in the hit film “Talladega Nights: The Ballad of Ricky Bobby,” which grossed over $150 million at the box office.
  • 2010: Ferrell founded the online comedy website Funny or Die, which features original sketches, parodies, and web series.
  • 2015: Ferrell starred in the HBO miniseries “The Spoils Before Dying,” which he co-wrote and produced.
  • 2018: Ferrell returned to Saturday Night Live to reprise his role as former President George W. Bush in a sketch about the Trump administration.
  • 2020: Ferrell starred in the Netflix film “Eurovision Song Contest: The Story of Fire Saga,” which he also co-wrote and produced.
