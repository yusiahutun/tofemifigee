How to Layer Clothes for Winter

June 2024 · 3 minute read

Winter forecasts can be nearly impossible to predict. One day it's freezing with snow on the ground, and the next, it's unseasonably warm. Instead of dressing for one extreme or another, expert Andrea Jagaric says layering clothes can help keep you prepared for anything and everything. "The chillier months naturally give us more options to play with when it comes to choosing what to wear for the day," says Jagaric before advising on the pieces to stock up on. "Look for options that are versatile and give you the option to mix and match for multiple different looks." For example, combining activewear with classic staples such as an oversized button-down or sherpa vest.

Meet the expert

  • Andrea Jagaric is the Chief Design Officer at Aerie.
  • Malissa Akay is the Executive Vice President and Chief Merchandising Officer at Express.

Layering also allows you to play around with the pieces that are already in your wardrobe. Give yourself permission to experiment with different textures, fabrics, weights, patterns, and colors, and the outfit possibilities are endless. And you don't have to be too concerned with going overboard—there are ways to layer clothes without looking bulky. Ahead, you'll find the best tips for layering clothes, complete with helpful advice from fashion professionals.

Add a Vest

According to Akay, a vest "allows you to have fun with statement sleeves and different collars." There is a variety of options to choose from, whether you go with something knitted, such as a sweater vest, or one that feels athleisure, like a puffer. "To add a bit of excitement to your look, we recommend pairing a vest with a balloon sleeve top or lightweight blouse that complements the specific style of the vest," says Akay.

You can use a vest to add a pop of color or amp up basic staples.

Slip On a Blazer

On especially chilly days, you might feel inclined to skip stiff jeans and formfitting tops for looser alternatives, such as baggy trousers or even sweats. You can quickly spruce up the loungewear by tossing on a tailored blazer. "By adding a blazer to your comfortable joggers and essential tee look, your outfit can be instantly transformed from loungewear to a polished and timeless classic," Akay says. The same tip applies to layering a blazer over a hoodie—not only will it look sleek and fashion-forward, but it's also a super warm outfit idea.

Try a Long Layer

There are plenty of ways to wear a dress during the winter months—adding tights, long boots, and slipping a turtleneck on underneath remain some of our favorite styling solutions. However, the duster coat has returned, and the trend is another way to pull together an outfit while also shielding your body from the cold.

Alternatively, you could try something shorter, such as a bomber, which Jagaric recommends. She adds that you can easily wear the piece underneath your bulky winter coat or trench, which will add flair and versatility. "This layering look not only brings dimension to your outfit but gives you the flexibility to take off your larger jacket needed for extra warmth and show off a more trend-driven piece underneath."
