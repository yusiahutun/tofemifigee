Which variety sorghum is best in Kenya?

The most popular variety in Kenya (in terms of marketability) is the GADAM variety. It is grey, and according to Kenya seed, it is tolerant to drought, pests and diseases, including foliar diseases, stem bores, bird invasion and more.

GADAM sorghum farming in Kenya is widespread as it matures faster – in just four months and has a higher yield per acre than other varieties. It's also the most preferred source of malt because of its rich taste and smell.

What is the ideal condition for sorghum production in Kenya?

Sorghum is a drought-resistant crop, meaning it'll do well in areas with a minimum of 420 mm annual rainfall. The crop requires relatively fertile land that's well-drained. Lastly, it can also grow in high altitude areas and places with high rainfall supply, but at this level, it is susceptible to pests such as downy mildew disease and shoot flies.

Where is the sorghum market?

The biggest market for sorghum in Kenya is the EABL through its subsidiary, East Africa Malting Limited. Sorghum is used in making the popular beer called keg, whose demand has steadily risen over the years after the Kenyan government banned illicit brews and reduced its excise duty.

But besides EABL, you can also sell it directly to consumers who will use it to make ugali, porridge, cookies, buns and more.

FAQs about sorghum farming in Kenya
