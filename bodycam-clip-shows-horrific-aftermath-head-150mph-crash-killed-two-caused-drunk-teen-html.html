Bodycam clip shows horrific aftermath of head-on 150mph crash that killed two caused by 'drunk' teen

June 2024 · 3 minute read

Bodycam footage has revealed the horrific aftermath of a head-on crash caused by a 'drunk' teen who was speeding at 150mph down the wrong lane in a Ford Mustang, killing two people. 

Police released a clip of the first officer frantically trying to comprehend the scene after Luke Christopher House, 18, slammed the sports car into an oncoming Chevy Impala at around 4am.

The passenger in House's Mustang Ryan Begnaud, an 18-year-old Oklahoma State University student, was found dead at the scene near State Highway 51 and Country Club Rd in Payne County.

Jeremi Smith, the 40-year-old passenger of the Chevy Impala, was also killed on impact. 

Virginia Winston, the 60-year-old woman driving the Impala suffered significant injuries and remains in the hospital.    

A traumatized witness is caught on camera telling the cop he'd seen a 'big old fire ball flip over' his car.

The cop dashes to House's Mustang - which landed on its side - before shouting: 'Hey anybody in there? Wake up.' 

He then approaches the red Impala, which is seen surrounded by debris, calling out: 'Are you alright? You okay?'  

Horror footage released by Stillwater Police shows the wreckage of Luke House's white Mustang which landed on its side in the road

The first officer on the scene is seen in bodycam footage frantically calling out for survivors in the two wrecked cars

Luke Christopher House, 18, in the mugshot released by police  

House survived the horror crash and was released from hospital the next day.

The extent of his injuries is unclear, but he was photographed for his mugshot with no visible marks on his face, neck or part of his torso.  

Several days later, he was charged with two counts of second-degree murder. 

The Oklahoma teen's Facebook profile shows clips of him revving the white muscle car - worth around $30,000 - and posing with his friends on the trunk as one flips the bird at the camera.

His posts have garnered scores of furious comments since the tragedy on October 15. 

One wrote: 'Mom & dad spent a lot of money on that car and supercharger just for you to Kill people with it being irresponsible.

'I hope they think about giving you the ability to have such a vehicle & they think about the lives of the people you took from earth because of your poor decisions in that car.'

Ryan Begnaud (left), the 18-year-old passenger in Luke House's Mustang, and Jeremi Smith (right), the 40-year-old passenger of the Chevy Impala, both died in the crash on October 15

A photo on Luke House's Facebook account shows three teens posing with similar white sports cars on a driveway while flipping the bird. The Mustang is believed to be the car involved in the horror crash, although it's unclear which of the three boys in this photo is House 

Another photo on 'drunk' teen driver Luke House's Facebook account shows what is thought to be the white Mustang he crashed killing two people

Another commented: 'You’re such a cool person with an awesome car. 

'Wish I could be as fearless as you must be to drive 150 mph in the wrong lane. 

'Life must feel pretty amazing to be carefree enough to drink and drive and not care about endangering others’ lives.'

Stillwater Police said both vehicles lost their engines in the dramatic crash. 

Lt. T.J. Low of the Stillwater Police Department described the crash a 'very sad deal'.

'The extent of the damages. The magnitude of it. It’s a pretty horrific crash scene.'
