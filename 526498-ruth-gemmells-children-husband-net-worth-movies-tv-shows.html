Early childhood 

Ruth Gemmell was born on October 1967 in Bristol, England, but was raised in Barnard Castle before her family relocated to Darlington County Durham upon her parents' divorce. She has three siblings, all boys, and attended Polam Hall School for early and elementary school education.

In pursuit of her childhood dream of acting, Gemmell moved to greater London. She attended the Webber Douglas Academy of Dramatic Art, which allowed her to reconnect with her father, who lived in the capital.

Career

After she graduated from the Webber Douglas Academy of Dramatic Art, Ruth landed her first major acting role in the Royal Shakespeare Company's adaptation, The Comedy of Errors. 

Following her success in the theatre, Ruth transitioned to television and film and landed her first role in Who Needs a Heart, followed by Band of Gold as the character Gina.
