15 best Lil Wayne songs

August 2024 · 9 minute read

Dwayne Michael Carter Jr, popularly known as Lil Wayne, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, record executive, entrepreneur and, an actor. He started his career in 1991 at the age of nine, when Bryan Williams (Birdman) discovered him and became the youngest member of Cash Money Records. Since then, the hip-hop sensation has released a series of hit and favourite albums that earned him international fame and raised his position to among the top international hip-hop artists. Lil Wayne songs have made over 100 million record sales globally.

Birdman is credited to have discovered young Wayne at the tender age of nine. Seeing how talented the young boy was, Birdman signed him to Cash Money Records. Lil Wayne happened to be among the youngest members in the label when signed. He collaborated with artistes in the label on various projects and dropped his solo debut album in 1999. The New Orleans native knows how to deliver what his fans want, and has always maintained a loyal fan base. Here are 15 best Lil Wayne songs.

1. Lollipop – Lil Wayne featuring Static Major

The iconic track “Lollipop” was the ultimate auto-tune masterpiece that arose at the height of the auto-tune movement and also the most popular Lil Wayne song. It sounds like a party about to fall off the edge of the Milky Way. It is this track that went beyond proving his brilliance to set the trend for so many other hip-hop artists. At that time, the song became a huge mainstay on Total Request Live television series spin cycle. The song became the number one selling ringtone of the year 2008, bumping out of millions of phone speakers nationwide. It instantly became one of Weezy’s top tracks on the charts.

3. A Milli – Lil Wayne

A Milli is the second single from the album ‘Tha Carter III’. With over 2 million worldwide sales, the song is one of his biggest hip-hop songs up to date. At one point, it seemed as if everyone in America had memorised every word of the song. On this track, Weezy goes on wordplay uninterrupted for four minutes while executing a fast flow but not sacrificing speed for lyricism. For a whole year, the song became an instant hit, playing non-stop at parties, on the radio and TV, and somehow until today, it still does't feel old.

4. 6 Foot 7 Foot – Lil Wayne featuring Cory Gunz

Lil Wayne spent eight months in the House of Detention for Men, the oldest prison on Rikers Island, whereby each cell is 6 feet by 8 feet by 7 feet which is one of the theories that explain the title of the song “6 Foot 7 Foot”. Lil Wayne is not always known for his lyricism, but the song changes everything. It served as a reminder that he is still one of the greatest rappers alive. The 2011 track was almost at the end of his long reign as the king of hip-hop for nearly half a decade, and he went out with more power as he came in within the music scene.
