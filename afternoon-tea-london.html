London's 10 best afternoon teas

March 2024 · 6 minute read

Courtesy of Claridge's

The Berkeley, Knightsbridge

Courtesy of The Berkeley

For the most fashionable tea in London, you really can't do better than The Berkeley's famous Prêt-à-Portea. This deliciously imaginative menu takes its inspiration from the world of fashion, with cakes and confections taking the form of up-to-date-season staples. This summer, you'll find designers including Kenzo and Moschino represented in confectionary, as well as Loewe's iconic 'Anthurium' dress reimagined as a Sacher sponge. Our favourite, though, has to be the Hermès tasselled bucket bag, crafted out of Victoria sponge sandwiched with apricot jam, wrapped in chocolate and finished with an indulgent chocolate feather plume.

The Berkeley, Wilton Place, SW1X 7RL

Claridge's, Mayfair

Courtesy of Claridge's

Could there be a more iconic venue for afternoon tea than the Claridge's foyer? The Art Deco hotel (pictured above in the main image) has long remained a top spot for this classic British pastime, with exemplary service and possibly the best cut sandwiches in London – all served on those signature Claridge's plates. Live piano music will accompany you as you are lulled into a Darjeeling-infused dream of an afternoon, with decadent patisserie, warm scones and, should you fancy it, a cool glass of bubbly. If you're a tennis fan, make booking tea here your top priority; from the 3 – 16 July, Claridge's are marking Wimbledon with a special tea to celebrate – just dust off your whites for the occasion...

Claridge's, Brook Street, W1K 4HR

Brown's Hotel, Mayfair

Courtesy of Browns Hotel

The charm of Brown's Hotel is undoubtedly its warm, cosy setting. With its wooden panelling and live piano music, it is a little like taking tea at home – if, of course, you have a particularly beautiful home and an exquisite pastry chef in your kitchen. The traditional English afternoon tea here consists of a choice of 17 blends, including Brown’s own concoction, and a selection of finger sandwiches, delicate pastries, fruit and plain scones with clotted cream and strawberry preserve. Top this off with a glass of Ruinart and you have the recipe for the perfect afternoon.

Brown's Hotel, 33 Albemarle St, W1S 4BP

The Prince Akatoki, Marble Arch

AMBIENTES VISUALES SAS

For an experience with a distinct difference, try the Koto Afternoon Tea at The Prince Akatoki in Marylebone, at which you can expect a menu full of fusion blends and authentic Japanese flavours. You’ll be serenaded with live Japanese music (played on the national instrument of Japan, the Koto – hence the tea’s name) as you indulge in out-of-this-world treats. Brioche with seared tuna and yuzu crème brûlée are both on the menu, as is a range of premium teas, including organic matcha – and, instead of the traditional champagne, you can opt for a sake pairing instead.

The Prince Akatoki, 50 Great Cumberland Place, W1H 7FD

Shangri-La The Shard, Bermondsey

Courtesy of Shangri-La The Shard

Available until the end of September, the 'Over The Rainbow' afternoon tea at Shangri-La The Shard's TĪNG Lounge is a seriously gourmet experience, with views of the city to match. Beneath a ceiling of clouds, you'll be treated to selection of delectable finger sandwiches served on a stand decorated with winding wildflower vines, plus more unusual savouries such as hibiscus flower and brioche burgers with ratatouille and green pesto. Of course, the classics also make an appearance: think freshly baked sweet and savoury scones, accompanied by strawberry jam and clotted cream. The unmissable icing on the cake (quite literally) is a miniature recreation of the Shard itself, filled with hazelnut praline, lemon jelly and Valrhona Dulcey mousse.

Shangri-La The Shard, 31 St Thomas St, SE1 9QU

The Connaught, Mayfair

Courtesy of The Connaught

Afternoon tea at Jean-Georges at The Connaught puts a playful, contemporary twist on the time-honoured British tradition. Curated art from Louise Bourgeois adorns the walls, while floor-to-ceiling windows, with their stained-glass accents by Jean-Michel Othoniel, give you the perfect vantage point to watch the world of Mayfair go by. The menu is just as opulent, with bold, South East Asian-inspired flavours blending perfectly with classic English recipes, from scones to finger sandwiches.

The Connaught, 16 Carlos Pl, W1K 2AL

11 Cadogan Gardens, Chelsea

AMBIENTES VISUALES SAS

Taking afternoon tea at 11 Cadogan Gardens, in the hidden depths of a glorious garden, feels like you're discovering Chelsea's best-kept secret. From the rose macaron to the rhubarb roulade, each intricate pastry, sweet treat and homemade savoury on the menu has been designed to evoke the flavours and aromas of the borough's most beautiful gardens. Peerlessly fluffy and light scones, strawberry jam, lemon curd, clotted cream and, of course, a refreshing glass of Laurent-Perrier Champagne are also included in the experience.

11 Cadogan Gardens, SW3 2RJ

Fortnum & Mason, Piccadilly

Courtesy of Fortnum and Mason

Not only can you buy your tea at this iconic department store, you can also sit back and relax in the prettily decorated, duck egg-blue Diamond Jubilee Tea Salon. The space has had many incarnations over the years – from the private apartment of the Fortnum's family, to the tea salon it is today, created to mark the 2012 jubilee. Here, you can choose from five different menus, which include fully vegetarian and gluten-free options and enjoy one of the most relaxed, yet classic, teas in town.

Fortnum & Mason, 181 Piccadilly, W1A 1ER

Cinnamon Bazaar, Covent Garden

Jodi Hinds

The acclaimed chef Vivek Singh has created a modern, Indian-inspired take on the classic afternoon tea at Cinnamon Bazaar, one of the capital's finest Indian eateries. The dishes take their cues from Vivek's Kolkata roots, with 'The High Chai' menu substituting finger sandwiches with kadhai-spiced paneer rolls and inventive chaat bites, followed by sweet delights such as shrikhand cheesecake. It is the ideal reimagining, washed down with a stunning collection of teas and, naturally, some bubbles.

Cinnamon Bazaar, 28 Maiden Lane, WC2E 7JS

The Goring, Victoria

David Cotsworth

As the holder of The British Tea Guild Council’s 'Top London Afternoon Tea' award, The Going has more than earned its spot on this list. A grand, five-star hotel (a favourite of the Royal Family, no less) with impeccable, old-world charm, The Goring is a timeless choice for an afternoon treat. The menu is ever-changing but reassuringly classic: freshly baked homemade scones served warm with Devonshire clotted cream and fruity homemade jam; delicious bakes, fancies and tarts; finger sandwiches and high quality tea blends that showcase the freshest seasonal ingredients. With over a century of experience, it's safe to say The Going understands what afternoon tea is all about.

The Goring, 15 Beeston Pl, SW1W 0JW
