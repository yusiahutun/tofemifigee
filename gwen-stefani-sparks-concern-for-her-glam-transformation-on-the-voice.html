Gwen Stefani sparks concern for her glam transformation on The Voice

Gwen Stefani has always been known for her bold fashion statements, but her recent ensemble on The Voice left fans and critics concerned.

The episode kicked off with Gwen setting the stage for an intense battle between two of her team members: Bias, an artist hailing from Little Rock, Arkansas, and Jacquie Roar, a single mother from Granada Hills, California.

 The two were tasked to sing Need a Favor by Jason Bradley DeFord, popularly known by his stage name, Jelly Roll, 38.

Throughout the coaching session, Gwen's choice of attire seemed to capture as much attention as the performances themselves. 

The iconic frontwoman of No Doubt sported a light blue oversized blazer with large white polka dots, paired with a matching dark blue tie bearing the same design. 

She complemented this with a white skirt featuring oversized light blue dots and a pair of striking black fishnet tights.

When Bias and Jacquie finally took the stage, their rendition was electrifying enough to give Niall Horan, 30, goosebumps, emphasizing the depth of talent this season.

Yet, Gwen's wardrobe shift during the judges' critique further amplified the discussion online. While seated in her signature red chair, she was seen wearing a voluminous white shirt with a prominent bow just beneath her chin. 

Topping it off was a black vest beneath the flowing fabric, leading a fan on X, the platform formerly known as Twitter, to comment that she resembled a "pirate."

The judges faced a tough decision. John Legend, 44, rooted for Bias; Reba McEntire, 68, backed Jacquie, while Niall again supported Bias. 

Amidst the tension and anticipation, Gwen confessed she "went with her heart," ultimately selecting Bias. 

However, the night wasn't over for Jacquie, as Reba promptly hit her red button, signaling her interest to recruit the talented singer for her team.

While the performers undoubtedly showcased their vocal prowess, the spotlight on social media veered towards Gwen's sartorial choices. 

Critics and fans alike had strong opinions. One viewer remarked: "Everything @gwenstefani wears on #thevoice makes her look immature and clownish." 

Another chimed in, commenting, "I’m sorry but that polka-dot outfit of Gwen’s is a no-go," which was met with a humorous retort: "It’s giving Donald Duck meets Minnie the Mouse vibes." Yet another quipped, "So that's where Seinfeld's puffy shirt went."
