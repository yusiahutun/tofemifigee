Wekiva River alligator attack witness details five minute struggle

June 2024 Â· 4 minute read

A witness who called 911 to report a horrific Florida alligator attack that left a woman with most of her arm ripped off said he saw the reptile wrestling with the victim for about five minutes.

Jakob Frick reported the moment when 37-year-old Rachel Lilienthal had her arm torn off above her elbow as she swam in the waters of the Wekiva River, a popular tourist spot, on Saturday.

In the 911 call, Lilienthal's blood-curdling screams can be heard in the background while Frick tries to remain calm and give information about the attack to a police dispatcher.

Frick said he witnessed the moment the alligator took off from the embankment towards Lilienthal, bit her arm off, pulled her underwater and wrestled for about five minutes, Click Orlando reported. 

'Holy s***, there's a lady that just got attacked by an alligator and it bit off her arm', Frick says to the dispatcher.

'I need you to calm down, I need you tell me where you are,' the dispatcher says.

'Yes it [the alligator] completely bit her arm off right at the shoulder,' he says as his conversation with the dispatcher continues.

He then explains that the victim is still in the water and that those helping her are trying to put her into a canoe. He also says that she is bleeding.

'You saw this woman, one minute swimming perfectly with both arms, a fit woman, and then all of the sudden she's flailing around with one arm,' Frick told Click Orlando.

He said moments before the incident he had been with a few friends as they were finishing up a canoeing trip on the Wekiva River.

One of his friends pointed out the no swimming signs and that alligators were in the area to Lilienthal, but she kept swimming, he said.

Wildlife officials say the woman had left the busiest part of the river, near the Wekiva Island recreation area, for a more secluded spot when 'something grabbed her arm'.

Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission spokeswoman Karen Parker told WFTV that Lilienthal, who is a Spanish lecturer at Rollins College, was also bitten on her back and her abdomen by the almost nine foot long reptile.

Parker said that as she swam to a remote, wooded part of the river, she felt the monster gator pull her under. 

The woman told rescuers that she wasn't sure how many times the 300-lb gator pulled her under, but that she felt it sever her arm.  

'You just saw some blood and some bone. There wasn't anything else there,' said eyewitness, Richard Ward to the Orlando Sentinel.

This blood-stained kayak is believed to have been involved in efforts to drag the woman to safety

Another couple who were kayaking nearby helped rescue Lilienthal by hitting the alligator with a paddle and pulling her into their kayak and out of the water. 

Paramedics arrived at the scene within 30 seconds, according to Frick. 

She was rushed to Orlando Regional Medical Center and underwent surgery.

Officials announced on Sunday that FWC trappers had captured the alligator around midnight after setting bait traps. 

The gator was immediately euthanized and disposed of. 

Officials told WFTV that they knew they had the right alligator because of evidence that was found in its stomach. 

The owners of Wekiva Island declined to comment. 

Alligator attacks in which somebody is badly wounded are relatively rare, with fewer than ten happening most years in Florida. The last fatality in Florida from a gator attack was in 2007.
