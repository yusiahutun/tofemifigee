HISTORICAL EVENTS

  • 1781: The city of Los Angeles is founded by Spanish governor Felipe de Neve.
  • 1886: The typewriter ribbon is patented by George Anderson.
  • 1957: The Ford Motor Company introduces the Edsel, an automobile brand that later becomes one of the biggest marketing failures in history.
  • 1998: Google is incorporated as a privately held company.
  • 2019: India’s Chandrayaan-2 successfully enters lunar orbit, making India the fourth country to achieve this feat.

SCIENTIFIC BREAKTHROUGHS

  • 1807: Robert Fulton’s steamboat, the North River Steamboat, makes its first successful trip from New York to Albany, revolutionizing transportation.
  • 1894: Antoine Becquerel discovers radioactivity while working with uranium salts.
  • 1951: The first live transcontinental television broadcast takes place in the United States, showing President Harry S. Truman’s speech.
  • 1972: Pioneer 10 becomes the first spacecraft to travel through the asteroid belt.
  • 1998: The first module of the International Space Station, the Zarya control module, is launched into orbit.

POLITICAL MILESTONES

  • 476: Romulus Augustus, the last Roman emperor, is deposed by Odoacer, a Germanic chieftain.
  • 1609: Navigator Henry Hudson discovers Manhattan Island.
  • 1781: The Los Angeles Pobladores, a group of 44 settlers from Mexico, establish the town of El Pueblo de Nuestra Señora la Reina de los Ángeles de Porciúncula, which later becomes Los Angeles.
  • 1957: Arkansas Governor Orval Faubus calls out the National Guard to prevent the integration of Little Rock Central High School, leading to the Little Rock Nine crisis.
  • 1985: The Achille Lauro cruise ship is hijacked by Palestinian terrorists in the Mediterranean Sea.

CULTURAL EVENTS

  • 1886: Geronimo, the leader of the Apache tribe, surrenders to the U.S. Army, marking the end of major Native American resistance in the Southwest.
  • 1957: The novel “On the Road” by Jack Kerouac, a defining work of the Beat Generation, is published.
  • 1971: John Lennon releases his solo album “Imagine,” featuring the iconic title track that becomes an anthem for peace.
  • 1995: The sitcom “Friends” premieres on NBC, captivating audiences and becoming one of the most popular TV shows of all time.
  • 2016: Beyoncé’s visual album “Lemonade” wins numerous accolades and receives critical acclaim for its powerful storytelling and music.

BIRTHS

  • 1859: Ivan Pavlov, Russian physiologist and Nobel laureate known for his work on classical conditioning.
  • 1928: Joan Nathan, American cookbook author and television personality specializing in Jewish cuisine.
  • 1949: Tom Watson, American professional golfer and winner of eight major championships.
  • 1960: Damon Wayans, American actor, comedian, and writer known for his roles in “In Living Color” and the “Lethal Weapon” film series.
  • 1981: Beyoncé Knowles, American singer, songwriter, and actress, recognized as one of the most influential women in the music industry.

NOTABLE DEATHS

  • 1609: Richard Burbage, English actor and member of the Lord Chamberlain’s Men, known for his association with William Shakespeare.
  • 1936: Harriet Monroe, American poet and founder of Poetry magazine, a significant publication in the Modernist poetry movement.
  • 1970: Alan Wilson, American musician and co-founder of the blues-rock band Canned Heat.
  • 1998: Mel Appleby, English singer and member of the duo Mel and Kim, known for their hit songs in the 1980s.
  • 2008: Anita Page, American actress and one of the last surviving Silent Film stars, best known for her role in “The Broadway Melody.”
