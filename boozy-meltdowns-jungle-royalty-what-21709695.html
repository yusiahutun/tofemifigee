Boozy meltdowns to topless snaps- what the EastEnders Branning girls did next

April 2024 · 6 minute read

The EastEnders Branning family came to prominence when Jim Branning (John Bardon) moved to Walford in the early 2000s following the death of his wife Reenie.

Since then, all six of Jim's children have appeared, many of them with family.

A new influx of Branning's joined the show between 2006 and 2012, including Max Branning and his wife Tanya and their children Lauren and Abi.

Max's son with his ex-wife Rachel was introduced in 2006 and Max and Tanya welcomed their third child in 2007.

While some of the family members left Walford as quick as they arrived, Max (Jake Wood) and Jack (Scott Marsden) are still holding the fort.

Some of the famous faces who played a role as part of the Branning clan have gone onto big things, some have left acting completely.

Madeline Duggan is best known for her portrayal of Lauren Branning from 2006 to 2010.

She was dropped from the series in May 2010 by executive producer Bryan Kirkwood and was replaced by Jacqueline Jossa.

Since leaving Walford, Maddie has gone onto star in several television shows as well as films and music videos.

The actress has racked up over 19,000 Instagram followers and often wows her fans with some saucy topless snaps from her holidays.

To mark her time on the show, Madeline shared a picture of her cheeky character posing in front of the Albert Square sign.

She wrote: "Apparently it's been 11 years since the Brannings arrived on Albert Square.

"It doesn't even feel like me, who was that 12 year old girl with the trademark side ponytail lol the MP3 really sets off the outfit."

Lauren Branning (played by Jacqueline Jossa)

Jacqueline Jossa may have been the second actress to play Lauren Branning but she certainly made her mark.

After an unforgettable eight years in the role, Jacqueline was written out alongside Lorna Fitzgerald, who played on screen sister Abi in 2018.

Luckily, the door has been left open for Lauren to return and Jacqueline always said she'd "never say never" to making a Walford comeback.

Since starting on the BBC One soap, Jacqueline has gone on to give birth to daughters Mia and Ella with TOWIE hunk Dan Osborne, has launchd a clothing range and was crowned the Queen of the Jungle in the 2019 series of I'm A Celebrity... Get Me Out of Here!.

This week, Jacqueline and her daughters teamed up with Billie Faiers and her daughter Nelly to launch a mother and daughter clothing range with In The Style.

Jacqueline raised some eyebrows last week as during Instagram Q&A with her 2.6 million followers, the mum-of-two was asked if she would play Max and Tanya's oldest daughter again.

She replied: Maybe one day. She's not dead yet."

Abi Branning (Played by Lorna Fitzgerald)

Lorna Fitzgerald made her debut as Abi Branning in 2006 and it was announced the actress was leaving the soap in 2017.

Fans were left heartbroken on 19 January 2018 as Abi plunged to her death after falling off the roof of the Queen Vic pub.

Since leaving the soap, Lorna, 23, has swapped the small screen for the stage and made her professional debut as Jackie Gimmick in Howard Brenton's The Shadow Factor in 2018.

She won acclaim for her debut performance and was shortlisted in the nominations for Best actress in a Play at The Stage Debut Awards.

Sustaining an early 20th century theme, Lorna starred as Iris Henderson in a stage version of Alfred Hitchcock's The Lady Vanishes.

Later in 2019 the actress was cast alongside Joan Collins and Matt Goss in the UK Comedy Drama Movie The Loss Adjuster.

Filming began in October 2019, with release due in December 2020.

Suzy Branning (played by Maggie O'Neill)

Suzy Branning was played by Julie Christian-Young for a brief appearance in 1996 and Maggie O'Neill took over the role in 2008.

The character was introduced as a love interest for Albert Square hard man Phil Mitchell (Steve McFadden) and made a strong love rival for Shirley Carter (Linda Henry).

Suzy left Walford during the Christmas Day episode and Maggie has gone onto star in Midsomer Murders, Call The Midwife and New Tricks.

More recently, she starred in the second series of ITV's Unforgotten and also played Yvonne in BBC drama The Split last year.

Kirsty Branning (played by Kierston Wareing)

Kierston Wareing played Max Branning's secret wife Kirsty for two years and made her explosive arrival on Christmas Day in 2012.

Speaking after her departure in 2014, Kierson told The Sun: "I didn’t believe in any of the storylines, it became ridiculous.

"I was always worried that I might be in EastEnders for ever, but they made that decision for me.”

The actress got involved in a row in a pub in a drunken meltdown in Essex in 2016.

Onlookers claim the Kierston said: "Don't you know who I am? I'm from the BBC" as door staff failed to recognise her outside The Sutton Arms in Hornchurch.

Another witness claimed that the actress became physically violent towards another onlooker during the incident.

A spokesperson for the TV star said: "She suffered an unprovoked attack, which resulted in a fractured arm and left her in severe pain so understandably she was upset.

"My client apologised that evening to anyone who she may have offended and will be reporting the attack to the police.”

Since leaving EastEnders, Kierston has starred in Hollyoaks and 100 Streets alongside Idris Elba.

April Branning (played by Debbie Arnold)

April arrived in Albert Square in December 1995 to invite Carol and her boyfriend Alan (Howard Anthony) to her wedding.

The "fun-loving" character departed after dumping Michael Rose and reuniting with her former lover to start a new life in Greece and was last seen on screen in 1996.

Actress Debbie Arnold was reportedly popular in the role of April, receiving 400 letters from fans in two weeks.

Debbie, 64, also played regular characters in Coronation Street, Hollyoaks, Emmerdale and Doctors meaning that she has appeared in every current British soap opera.

In 2017, Debbie accused Michael Winner of sexual harassment, prompted by the Harvey Weinstein scandal.

Alongside fellow actress Cindy Marshall-Day, Debbie appears don This Morning where they claimed Winner asked them to expose their breasts during auditions early in their careers.
