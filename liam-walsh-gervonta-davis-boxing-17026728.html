EXCLUSIVE: Liam Walsh reveals all on father, family life, and ditching beer for Scrabble

October 2024 · 5 minute read

They are the winning characters he wants as he takes on IBF super-featherweight champion Gervonta Davis at the Copper Box Arena in London.

Walsh is not an ordinary boxer. Not many fighters challenging for a world title drive an old and battered Volkswagen Touran and play Scrabble in the evenings to relax.

While American Davis’ has a remarkable tale to tell of escaping the crime-ridden streets of Baltimore, Walsh’s story is very different.

This is a fighter who cites sugar as the only drug he has ever taken as not even caffeine or alcohol passes his lips.

“I just don’t like the taste of beer,” said Walsh. “I can’t drink 10 pints of water so how the hell am I going to drink 10 pints of fizzy?

“The worst thing I do is when I’m not training for a fight on a Saturday after football, I go with my twin Ryan to the bar and we have half a can of coke and half a Mars bar each. That is the treat of the week.”

He is so close to his family that he and wife Sarah live next door to his twin brother Ryan and their older brother Michael on a plot of land in Cromer, Norfolk. Their house even has a connecting door with Ryan’s.

They are all raising their own families where they moved to from Rochdale when Liam was nine after their parents – John and Michelle – told them they were just going to the area for their holidays.

“Me and Ryan are with each other every single second of the day, every day,” said Walsh. “The only time we won’t be together is when we sleep.

“We train together all day and then we will have a game of Scrabble in the evening.

“We play it most evenings. It’s a bit different but we enjoy it and helps us relax. It’s not a usual hobby for boxers.

“If I bump into anyone who knows me and I’m not with Ryan they go ‘where’s Ryan’ or vice versa.

“For me it’s our biggest achievement (staying so close) and our dad will be proud of us, wherever he is, knowing that we have kept that tightness. He insisted on it from a young age.

“If Ryan and Michael quit boxing then I’m out. I don’t think I can do it on my own. We grind together and we shine together.”

Dad John, who predicted Liam would be a world champion before he had picked up a pair of gloves, died in 2011 from a heart attack.

“At the time I thought ‘this is really bad, it’s the end of the world and why me’,” said Walsh.

“Then I had a friend who lost his dad and he had to see him in a hospital bed for six months slowly dying. I thought that’s hard, that’s really hard.

“Staying so close (as a family) is our biggest achievement and our dad will be proud of us – wherever he is – knowing that we have kept that tightness.

“Any situation we have had in life, we are together. That strength is hard to explain unless you’re in that situation.

“The shoes I have, the clothes I have on my back, these are my brothers’ clothes.

“If I got £99, that’s £33 for Ryan, £33 for Michael and £33 for me. That’s just how it is.

“I don’t have my own money, everything we have is ours.

“When I win this world title it’s for them too.”

So dedicated is Walsh that he left home for five days sparring in Wales as Sarah went into labour with their fourth child.

The reward for that focus will be huge if he overcomes the odds, but he says victory won’t change him.

“I will still be Liam from Cromer just walking down the street and I’ll still have my clapped out motor with dents all over it,” he said.

“I could earn a billion and I wouldn’t change. I’ve never been motivated by money. I believe I’m the happiest man alive because of the way I am.”

It’s a sharp contrast to Davis’ promoter Floyd Mayweather Jr, a man who nicknamed himself ‘Money’.

Walsh was once a fan and travelled to Las Vegas to watch Mayweather, but says: “I’m not in awe of Floyd Mayweather the man, I’m not in awe of any man.

“I’ve admired what he has done in the ring but I’ve never been star struck. He is not a better man than me.

“He doesn’t impress me much as a man anyways, there’s people out there saving lives working for the Ambulance service, fire service, these are proper heroes. A boxer is not a hero.

“It shocks me how you can be knighted for hitting a tennis ball or boxing.

“My missus gave birth the other day and there are people fussing around her making sure the baby comes out OK. Those are the type of people who should be knighted.”

While he will care little for the money and the plaudits he will receive if he wins, Walsh will reward himself with a holiday.

Even though he insists he will still train every day while away.

But first the other addiction he has will be satisfied.

“I think I will celebrate with a cake,” he added. “There’s a nice cake factory near us.”
