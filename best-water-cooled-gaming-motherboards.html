When planning to build a powerful gaming PC, one of the biggest challenges is dealing with temperatures. With many new CPUs managing to reach soaring heat when put under strain, the last thing you want is to experience frame drops due to your system overheating.

One way to deal with overheating issues is to build a custom loop. Motherboards with built-in water blocks make keeping your CPU and VRM cooler easier by including them in your custom loop. But there are a few challenges, firstly, there are not as many motherboards built with water cooling built-in, but also, when you find a good one, they are generally more expensive than standard motherboards.

So, to help you pick the right motherboard for your custom-built PC, These are the five best water-cooled motherboard gaming available today.

FAQ

Q: Is Water Cooling Better Than Fans?

Water cooling distributes heat much more efficiently than traditional fans. Not only does this make them much more efficient coolers, but it also reduces the need for fans to work as hard, which can result in an overall much quieter gaming PC.

Q: Do You Need to Refill a Liquid-Cooling System?

If you are using an AIO (all-in-one) or a closed loop, then no, you should not need to change the liquid. However, if you are using an open loop, you do need to flush and refill your liquid coolers, which should be done yearly to ensure the cooling remains efficient.
