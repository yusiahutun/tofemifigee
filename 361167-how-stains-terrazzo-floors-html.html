How do you get stains out of terrazzo floors

March 2024 · 8 minute read

Terrazzo is a composite material of chips, either from granite, marble, glass, quartz, or shells mixed with binders such as cement, resin matrix, or epoxy matrix to create a luxurious and smooth floor, wall or countertop. Terrazzo floors are widely common and available because of their durability and beauty though considered expensive to install and maintain. So, how do you get stains out of terrazzo floors?

Although the aggregate materials used in the terrazzo floor are durable, the binders used are usually porous and can get easily stained. Cement-based terrazzo floors are more susceptible because cement is more porous among the binders. Just like other surfaces, terrazzo floors need regular maintenance to keep them dirt-free, clean, and shiny.

How do you get stains out of terrazzo?

Each type of terrazzo floor needs different maintenance methods applied to them, and it is crucial that you correctly identify the type of terrazzo floor you have.

Terrazzo floors are durable and beautiful as long as harsh chemicals and dirt do not get poured on them. Avoid using the wrong cleaning method or cleaning solution that may pose problems or even permanent damage on the floor. So, do you know how to deep clean terrazzo floors and which method to use?

Here are the simple steps to deep clean terrazzo floors and remove stains.

  • Remove the crumbs, debris, and loose dirt by sweeping the floor. Use a dry mop if the floor is too dusty.
  • Use a neutral PH cleaner that is non-acidic and non-alkaline mixed with plain water. Mix the neutral cleaner with water according to instructions by the manufacturer. Mop to wet clean the entire floor.
  • Allow the cleaner to settle on for a couple of minutes to dissolve the caked dirt. Make sure all parts of the floor remain wet to avoid loose dirt drying back onto the floor again.
  • Use clean water to rinse the floor thoroughly. Repeat several times to remove all dirt if the floor was too dirty.
  • Allow the floor top air-dry overnight after removing all dirt and cleaner.
  • Now apply a terrazzo sealer from a bucket. Use an appropriate applicator such as a floor roller or a brush. Remove the excess sealer and again allow to dry overnight.

    Cleaning specific stubborn stains off terrazzo floors

    So, how do you get the stubborn stains out of your terrazzo floors? Can you use bleach on terrazzo floors? To answer you, here is how you approach the stubborn stains and how to remove them.

    Note that whenever you're cleaning your terrazzo floor, avoid using bleaches, abrasives cleaners, ammonia, or vinegar directly on the floor to remove any dust, debris, or dirt.

    Using bleach will discolour your floor and also remove the shine off it. As the terrazzo floor is porous, the bleach will get absorbed into the cement and start to break down and damage the floor. So, how do you get stains out of terrazzo floors? Here's how to handle different types of stains.

    1. Removing a coffee stain

    To remove the stain, mix three parts of plain water with one part of glycerin and soak an absorbent cloth in it. Cover the affected area with the damp cloth and allow it to settle for a couple of minutes then clean off.

    Alternatively, you can try making a paste by mixing calcium carbonate and hydrogen peroxide or hot water and abrasive powder and apply on the affected surface.

    2. Getting rid of an ink stain

    Use liquid detergent mixed with warm water to try and remove the ink stain. If it is stubborn, apply a solution of water and ammonia using a cotton ball or absorbent cloth to wipe out the stain until it all comes out.

    If the ink stain has stayed for long on the floor, make a paste using water and two tablespoons of sodium perborate. Apply the paste on the stain and rinse off after a while.

    If the floor is affected with stubborn blue ink, try using a strong solution of ammonia and water-soaked on an absorbent cloth and placed over the stain.

    3. Cleaning a bloodstain

    Try to wipe off using plain cold water. If persistent, use a few drops of ammonia mixed in water and apply on the stain. Allow to settle on for about fifteen minutes and rinse off.

    Alternatively, drizzle a few drops of lukewarm water on the stain and sprinkle some malt powder on it. Give it a couple of minutes to an hour to settle and then rinse off.

    4. Clearing a soft drink patch

    Use hydrogen peroxide saturated on an absorbent cloth and placed over the stain. Follow this by placing another absorbent cloth soaked in ammonia over the stain. Allow a couple of minutes to settle and then wipe out.

    5. Removing an alcohol spill

    Use hot water mixed with liquid detergent to get rid of the alcohol stain. If it is persistent, try dabbing it with denatured alcohol. If it is still stubborn, use an absorbent cloth soaked in hydrogen peroxide and place on the affected area, followed by another fabric, saturated with ammonia.

    6. Paint or oil stain removal

    To get paint off terrazzo floor or any oil stain, use warm water mixed with liquid detergent to clean off the affected surface gently.

    7. Getting rid of urine

    To get the terrazzo thoroughly clean, mix hot water and abrasive powder to make a paste and apply it on the surface affected. Allow a couple of minutes to settle and wash off.

    For the stubborn urine stain, try using a cloth soaked in hydrogen peroxide and lay it over the affected surface. Follow by laying another cloth saturated with ammonia over the other cloth and again allow settling for a few minutes before washing off.

    8. Removing rust

    For less severe rust stain, use an absorbent cloth soaked in hydrogen peroxide and place it over the stain. Follow by placing another absorbent cloth soaked in a solution of water and ammonia and place it over the other cloth. Let them settle on the stain for about an hour before you can remove and wipe the surface. Repeat the process if the rust remains.

    If the rust has not come out after repeating, or the stain covers a more significant area, try using a poultice cleansing paste. Make the paste by mixing six parts of water and a part of sodium citrate. Add six parts of glycerin and one or two parts of calcium carbonate.

    Mix all the items to a thick paste that can stick on the surface when applied. Generously apply the paste on the stain and leave for three to five days. Close off the affected area from traffic to allow the paste to work on the stain.

    Afterward, use any degreasing floor cleaner and wash off the paste. If this method is not working, you will need to strip, seal, and wax the entire floor a fresh to get rid of the stain.

    Steam cleaning terrazzo floors

    It is advisable to steam clean terrazzo floors periodically only when there is caked-on debris. Otherwise, regularly use a damp mop and a recommended cleaning solution such as neutral PH cleaners to clean your terrazzo floor.

    If it is possible, prepare your room by removing all the furnishings from the room before you can steam clean it. Moving them will prevent your furnishings from getting damp from the steam. Now thoroughly sweep the entire room and ensure no debris remains to avoid getting them wet with the steam and they stick to the floor.

    Fill your steam cleaner with water, precisely the amount recommended by the manufacturer. Use tap or distilled water and avoid water with a lot of minerals. Also, don’t add any cleaning solution, just use plain water.

    Plug your steam cleaner to the power outlet and turn it on. When the ‘Ready’ light turns on, place the steam cleaner to the corner of the house and release the steam by pressing the trigger on the steam cleaner. Carefully pass the steam cleaner evenly over the floor for about eight to fifteen seconds and no more. When done, press the trigger again to stop releasing the steam. The steam from the cleaner with both clean and sanitise the floor.

    Ensure you don’t leave the steam cleaner on one position for a long time as the steam will cause a residue buildup. In case it happens, use a white vinegar saturated cloth to remove the residue. You should avoid using the steam cleaner if your floor is not with a commercial sealer. If you do, moisture will be absorbed into the floor and cause it to crack.
