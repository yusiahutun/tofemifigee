She's known for performing in elaborate outfits - and plenty of them.

And Cher decided to wind back the clock to the 1970s by donning a look from more than 40 years ago during her Monday night show in Philadelphia.

The 67-year-old pop icon, who's currently performing her Dressed To Kill tour dates in the US, took to the stage in a traditional Native American inspired get-up - just like the one she wore in her 1973 music video for Half-Breed (and subsequent tour).

If she could turn back time! Cher wound the clock back to 1973 this week when she performed in an outfit inspired by the traditional Native American look she first wore more than 40 years ago

The flamboyant outfit included an enormous colourful head dress that almost touched the grown as the singer crossed the stage in one of over a dozen costume changes at the Philadelphia concert.

A two-piece ensemble of a triangle-shaped top and a strip of material that hung from her waist made of embellished, colourful fabric left plenty of the singer's skin on show.

Indeed despite more than 40 years passing since the black-haired beauty wore the original costume, she still looked fantastic and trim in the modern day reincarnation.

She's still got it! The 67-year-old wasn't afraid to show some skin despite four decades passing since she wore the original costume

Cher also recycled another famous and obviously beloved stage costume for the Dressed To Kill tour.

A racy black body suit she memorably wore to perform at London's Wembley arena in 1992 has made made a comeback - and it's not the first time.

The artist also pulled the dominatrix-style number back out of the archives to sing at a charity dinner in New York City in 2003.

Remember me? The pop icon also donned a dominatrix-style black bodysuit on the Dressed To Kill tour, having worn the exact same piece to several high profile events over the years

Firm favourite: Cher wore the sheer sequined bodysuit paired with a leather jacket and knee-high boots to perform at a charity dinner in New York in 2003, and to the MTV Music Video Awards in 2010

Nothing to hide! She also wore the daring number to perform at London's Wembley Stadium in 1992

Yet again in 2010, the confident starlet wore the skimpy piece once at the MTV Music Video Awards - although this time she added a touch of modesty with a black leather jacket (or maybe it was just cold).

The star's other costumes on the Dressed To Kill tour - which guest stars fellow singing icon Cyndi Lauper - are a sight to behold.

One particularly elaborate costume looks to be insprired by Cleopatra, with an excessively large head dress sitting atop a wig of heavy black plaits.

Another appears to be Cher's take on a medieval princess, a draped pink gown accessoried with glittering jewels and an oversize head piece.
