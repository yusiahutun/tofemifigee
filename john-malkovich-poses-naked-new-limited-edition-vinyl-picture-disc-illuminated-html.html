John Malkovich poses naked for new limited edition vinyl picture disc, Illuminated

April 2024 · 2 minute read

He's well known for baring his sole in his myriad movie roles.

Now John Malkovich has gone a step further, stripping naked for the cover art for 12-inch single picture disc, Illuminated, that has a 1,500 limited edition.

The tracks are taken from the 62-year-old's 2015 album, Like A Puppet Show, according to the Vinyl Factory on Wednesday.

Letting it all hang out: John Malkovich stripped naked for the cover art for his new 12-inch, limited edition vinyl picture disc, Illuminated

For the album, composer Eric Alexandrakis recorded a set of ambient tracks and asked the Oscar-nominated actor to recite Plato’s Allegory Of The Cave over the music.

Eric then sent the tracks on to artists including Yoko Ono and her son Sean Lennon, Frank Zappa's son Dweezil and Sameer Ghadia of Young The Giant to reinterpret as they saw fit.

Now, some of those songs are being issued as a 12-inch vinyl picture disc with John, posing as Rodin's The Thinker statue, naked on one side and suited up on the other.

Cover shot: The 62-year-old suited up when he posed as Rodin's The Thinker statue on one side of the disc

In his birthday suit: The Oscar-nominated star left little to the imagination in this shot. Both were taken by photographer and director Sandro Miller.

The image was photographed by Sandro Miller, who is also directing a short film, Allegory Of The Cave, starring John.

Allegory Of The Cave will premiere at the Cannes Film Festival that runs from May 11 to 22 this year, according to the Vinyl Factory.

Meanwhile John has been busy directing A Celebration Of Harold Pinter at off-Broadway venue the DR2 Theatre, which he first helmed in 2012.

Promo tour: Musician Eric Alexandrakis, left, John and photographer-director Sandro Miller, signed copies of their album, Like A Puppet Show, at a record store last November

He reunites with Julian Sands, who stars in the one-man show.

Julian developed it after the playwright, who died in 2008, asked him to prepare a set of his poems for a special presentation in London, according to Playbill.

The play opened March 15 and is due to run until April 3.
