List of radio stations in Kenya and their frequencies

February 2024 · 11 minute read

Over the years, Kenya has remained the centre of East Africa where most of the businesses and economic activities are conducted in the region. As a result, the number of radio stations in Kenya is skyrocketing to meet the high demand for quick and reliable means of communication. This comes after the attainment of the freedom of expression and the airwaves in the 1990s. Like in most other countries, Kenyans enjoy listening to the radio for entertainments news and talk show conversations that allow them to continue with their normal activities. This guide provides the list of the most popular radio stations in Kenya and their frequencies to let you find your favorite radio station.

Besides the large government and private-controlled organizations and companies that produce the most widespread newspapers in Kenya, there are several television stations and over 140 radio stations in the country. Most of these are online radio stations in Kenya that make use of the latest Internet technology. With the cost of getting licenses to run FM radio stations in Kenya getting cheaper, there is a high increase in the number of vernacular radio stations in Kenya that reach most of the communities in the country.

Christian radio stations in Kenya

There are several Christian radio stations in Kenya that focus on reaching their audience with the gospel of Jesus Christ. These stations are spread all over the country with some of them having nationwide coverage. Nevertheless, even those that are confined to a particular area can still be reached online since most of them are part of the online radio stations in Kenya. Here are some of the leading Christian radio stations in Kenya based on the area they cover.

1. Hope 93.3 FM

This radio station first went on air in 2003 and broadcasts in different locations in Kenya with its frequency being 93.3. Some of its popular programmes include Praise Junction, Hope for Today, Breath of Heaven and Activate.

2. Family Radio 103.9 FM (Radio 316)

Also called Radio 316, Family Radio 103.9 FM is one of the leading Christian radio stations in Kenya based in Nairobi and was founded in the late 1990s. It is live on air for 24 hours seven days a week and features modern day gospel music and other Christian content like sermons and talk shows.

3. Bibilia Husema Broadcasting (BHB) FM 102.5

BHB 102.5 FM is based in Nairobi and was established to promote Christianity in Kenya. It features various programmes including Christian teachings, music, and preaching.

4. Truth FM 90.7

With the aim of spreading the word of God in Kenya and abroad, Truth FM 90.7 is under the ministry of African Inland Church Kenya. It is live on air for all the hours of the day every week and broadcasts in English for more than half of the time.

5. Imani Radio 88.8 FM

Appearing on the list of radio stations in Kenya, Imani Radio 88.8 FM is a Christian radio station broadcasting from Kitale. It features various popular programmes like Merikebu, Blessed Moments and Baraka Rizikini.

The list of Christian radio stations in Kenya is endless with several stations being established every year. Their frequencies can also vary from region to region, but most of them are usually limited to a particular audience and area.

Hip hop radio stations in Kenya

Here is a list of top hip hop radio stations in Kenya alongside their frequencies:

1. Homeboyz Radio (HBR) 103.5 FM

This is a famous radio station in Nairobi that features hip hop music and various entertainment programmes. Some of the notable HBR 103.5 FM programmes include the morning Gmoney, Uptown Nights, Trinity Connect, The Juice and Jam Session.

2. Campus Radio

Campus radio is one of the hip hop radio stations in Kenya that broadcast primarily online. It is a favourite station for most city youths and students because of the type of music it plays and the sheng language used most of the time.

3. Kiss 100 FM 100.3

Kiss 100 FM with its frequency being 100.3 and 100.0 in some places is a leading web platform that mainly features information and entertainment. It has a wide coverage countrywide and online due to its contemporary hit songs.

    Though the freedom of media and freedom of expression are guaranteed in Kenya, all media houses including the radio stations in Kenya are regulated by a government body called the Media Council of Kenya. This is an independent institution established according to the Media Act of 2007 to monitor the conduct and discipline of journalists in Kenya. Hence, all radio stations in Kenya are allowed to go on air only when they have met the stipulated requirements of the Media Council of Kenya. This mainly depends on the size of the radio station with the biggest radio stations in Kenya having stringent requirements to meet.
