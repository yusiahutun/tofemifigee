Best Sandals For Walking - Most Comfortable Travel Shoe

August 2024 · 3 minute read

I've owned the same pair of Birkenstocks since 2002. I've been a fan of Saltwater sandals for even longer. But I've always been on the lookout for a sandal that is as chic as it is comfortable. While I love my Birks and Saltwaters, those sandals are a little bit more hippie and or toddler-chic than a lot of occasions require.

So, after reading about these so-called grandma sandals that looked cute but "feel like sneakers," I decided to take the plunge and try out a pair of Charleston Shoe Co.'s Monterey sandal in black.

Charleston Shoe Co.

SHOP NOW Monterey Sandal, Charleston Shoe Co., $100

When they first arrived, I was worried that the size eight I ordered was a little too long in the toe and that the thick elastic ankle straps would be too tight since they cut into my skin creating little bit of bulge above and below the strap. Thankfully, that awkward ankle bulge went away as soon as I walked around in them for a few hours. While the elastic stretched out enough to make it comfortable to wear them, they've retained enough elasticity to stay on my feet better than pretty much any other sandal I've ever owned.

This style comes in over 15 colors, but the chunky black rubber sole is the same on all the shoes adding a 90s-chic element to your outfits (trust me, it's in again now).

While the low block heel adds about two inches of height, the thick platform evens it out so it feels like you're wearing flats. The suede foot bed is buttery soft, but most importantly it doesn't make your feet sweat like other leather-lined sandals, making it possible to wear these all day long, even on long walks or running errands.

The only downside? It did take about a week of breaking them in before the blisters on my big toes went away, but it was nothing that a couple of Band-Aids couldn't fix.

After wearing them for a month, they quickly become my go-to shoe this summer. Nearly every time I wore them out with my friends, I got compliments about how cute they were and questions about whether they were comfortable too.

One time, I even got cat-called by a man while wearing them. Usually I would be annoyed by such an obnoxious intrusion into my quiet walk home, but his simple, "Hey, I like your shoes," caught me off-guard since I never expected strangers—let alone men—to find these "grandma sandals" appealing.

Since I travel a lot for work, I can also vouch that these chunky sandals are the ideal travel shoe. With no buckles or fussy laces (I'm looking at you, espadrilles), they're easy to get on and off while going through security at the airport. Another plus? They go with everything from boyfriend jeans on the weekend to black trousers at work and cute dresses at night, making it easy to just pack one pair of shoes and save lots of room in my luggage.

True story: A few weeks ago, I spotted a woman running through JFK airport wearing a pair of red Monterey sandals. Not only did she not trip while sprinting to make her flight, she also looked impossibly chic. Now I might need a red pair too.
