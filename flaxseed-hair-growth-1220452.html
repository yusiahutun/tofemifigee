What is Flaxseed?

 Popularly known as ‘atasi’ in Ayurveda, flaxseeds are oval-shaped seeds which come from flax plants or linseeds. Enriched with the goodness of omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, proteins, Vitamins B and E, and fiber, the powerful plant food offers numerous benefits for overall wellness (1). Our contributor Natalie Xerri, says, “Some studies have demonstrated that omega 3 fatty acids can provide benefits for hair growth. Flaxseed is one of the highest sources of omega 3 and let's face it, it's much more appealing to have flaxseed as a hair product ingredient than other non-vegan resources.” The versatile superfood is lauded for several health benefits such as providing protection against heart disease, curing diabetes, and alleviating symptoms of cancer.

How Is Flaxseed Good for Hair Growth? 

We’ve all heard time and again how flaxseed contributes to a nutritious and beautiful hair care regime. The plant food serves various purposes, one of which turns out to be a remedy against all your hair care fuss. Here’s how.

Goodness of Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Flaxseeds contain Omega-3 fatty acids that work as a boon for healthy locks. The omega-3 fatty acids provide your hair follicles with an essential dose of proteins and nutrition. It also promotes circulation in the scalp, inhibiting inflammation of hair follicles which is a big factor behind hair thinning and hair loss (2).

Enriched With Vitamin B

Flaxseeds are a rich source of Vitamin B, biotin, riboflavin, niacin, pyridoxine, folic acid, and pantothenic acid, all of which contribute towards the enhancement of healthy hair. Studies have pointed out how the deficiency of Vitamin B is related to hair loss (3). The inclusion of flaxseeds in your wellness routine can alleviate symptoms of hair loss. 

Loaded With Fiber

Replete with fiber, flaxseed is good for hair as the presence of fiber works to strengthen your locks at its core. Flaxseeds have a sufficient amount of fiber in them that acts as your hair’s favorite pal when it comes to replenishing them. 

Rich Source of Vitamin E

The powerful antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties of Vitamin E diminish the effect of free radicals on your scalp and strengthen hair follicles making flax seeds for hair an excellent solution (4). Vitamin E also prevents premature graying of your locks due to oxidative stress (5).

Abundance of Proteins 

Protein is a significant factor for hair growth and flax seeds have a high proportion of protein in them (6). For all of you who are vegetarians, or vegan, flax seeds make for an excellent gluten-free source of protein. Add some of this plant-based product to your everyday diet and reap flaxseed hair growth benefits.

Might Balance Scalp pH Level

Anecdotal evidence suggests that flaxseeds might help control and balance the pH level of your scalp by calming the sebaceous glands and ensuring the production of an adequate amount of oil by them. This can further help keep your scalp healthy, promoting improved hair texture and growth. 

Excellent Source of Alpha-linolenic Acid(ALA)

Flaxseeds are one of the richest plant sources of alpha-linolenic acid aka ALA (7). Anecdotal evidence points to how the acid might soothe scalp irritations, especially those which are associated with eczema or psoriasis. 

Moisture Binding 

The presence of essential vitamins in flaxseed work towards the nourishment of hair follicles and ensures enhanced hydration of your locks as well. By sealing the cuticles of your strands, the powerful ingredient minimizes the risk of split ends and damage.

How to Effectively Use Flaxseeds for Your Hair

The versatile ingredient is mostly available in all of our pantries, ready for use. It’s extremely effortless to whip your own DIY flaxseed hair care recipes, free from all the harsh chemicals within minutes. Here are some of the most effective and easy-to-prepare flax seeds for hair recipes. 

Flaxseed Gel

Flaxseed gel can work amazingly well for all hair types, but it’s recommended most for curly locks. 

Essentials Required

  • Flaxseeds
  • Aloe vera
  • Tea tree essential Oil
  • Lemon juice
  • A glass jar
  • Spoon
  • Knee-length stockings

Processing Time

1.5 hour 

Steps

  • Take a medium size bowl and add 2.5 cups of water to it.
  • Pour 1⁄4 cup of flax seeds into it and boil the water for approximately 5-7 minutes. Keep stirring while the water is boiling so that the flaxseeds do not end up stuck to the base of the pot.
  • Put some drops of lemon juice in the container. Then, turn off the gas stove and let the gel cool for approximately an hour. 
  • Then take a knee-length stocking in a measuring cup and pour the thick gel into it. 
  • Strain the gel by transferring it from the stocking to the measuring cup.
  • Add a few drops of fresh aloe vera gel and tea tree essential oil to enhance the effectiveness of the gel.
  • Now, pour your flaxseed gel into a glass jar with an airtight lid. 
  • Refrigerate the gel and use it daily for styling your hair and enhancing your scalp’s health. 

Flaxseed Oil for Hair

Essentials Required

Advertisement
  • A good quality flaxseed oil
  • A hot towel

Processing Time

40-45 minutes

Steps

  • Get premium quality all-natural flaxseed oil and warm it in a bowl for a few seconds. 
  • Gently massage the oil into your scalp.
  • Divide your locks into sections and apply the oil to the complete length. 
  • Then, dip a towel in hot water, twist it well, and wrap it around your hair.
  • Let it stay for 30 minutes.
  • Take a wide-tooth comb and detangle all the knots.
  • Wash your hair with a mild sulfate-free shampoo.

The heat from both the oil and hot towel allows the goodness of the flaxseeds to penetrate your locks including the roots and scalp with ease. Repeat the process at least 2-3 times a week to reap the maximum benefits of flaxseed oil for hair. 

Flaxseed Hair Mask

Essentials Required

  • Flaxseeds
  • Raw honey
  • Banana
  • Olive oil
  • Lavender essential oil

Processing Time

10 minutes

Steps

  • Take two tablespoons of flax seeds and grind them well into a fine powder.
  • Take a banana, chop it into pieces, and add those pieces to the powder. 
  • Add a tablespoon of raw honey to the mix.
  • Then, add a tablespoon of olive oil to your mask.
  • Add a few drops of lavender essential oil to enhance the aroma of your mask, especially if you’re not fond of the banana’s smell. 
  • Divide your hair into sections. Then, apply the mask to your scalp and complete the length of your locks.
  • Let the mask sit for about two hours. 
  • Wash your hair with lukewarm water. 

Add the DIY hair mask to your routine and make the most of flaxseeds. 

Flaxseed Hair Conditioner

Hair conditioning is a step that all beauty gurus vouch for. And there’s nothing better than an all-natural hair conditioner that will suit all hair types and provide the benefits of flaxseed for hair.

Advertisement

Essentials Required

  • Flaxseed
  • Aloe vera gel
  • Virgin coconut oil
  • Tea tree essential oil

Processing Time

10-15 minutes

Steps

  • Take a bowl and add a cup of water to it.
  • Boil the water on low flame for a few minutes and add three tablespoons of flaxseeds to it.
  • Stir the mixture well to form a gel-like consistency.
  • Turn off the stove and let the gel cool down for a few minutes.
  • Take a strainer and separate the leaves from the liquid. 
  • Then, add 3 tablespoons of aloe vera gel to the liquid.
  • Add 2 tablespoons of virgin coconut oil to the mix.
  • Pour 2-3 drops of tea tree essential oil into the container. 
  • Refrigerate the prepared conditioner. 
  • Apply it to your damp locks and let it stay for 510-15 minutes.
  • Wash with lukewarm water. 

Flaxseed Hair Pack

An anti-hair fall flaxseed hair pack is one DIY recipe that’s a hit since the majority of us deal with hair fall on an on-and-off basis. 

Essentials Required

  • Flaxseed oil
  • Onion juice 
  • Aloe vera gel

Processing Time

5 minutes

Steps

  • Take 3 tablespoons of flaxseed oil for hair and warm it for a few minutes.
  • Add a tablespoon of onion juice to the lukewarm oil.
  • Then, add a tablespoon of fresh aloe vera gel to the mix.
  • Mix the ingredients well to prepare the pack.
  • Apply the pack from the roots of your hair to the tip.
  • Leave it for 15-20 minutes.
  • Wash your hair with a mild shampoo.

Flaxseed With Yogurt

Make yourself a mouth-watering flaxseed and yogurt treat in minutes. 

Essentials Required 

  • Low-fat Yogurt
  • Strawberries
  • Blueberries
  • Flax seeds
  • Cinnamon

Processing Time

5 minutes

Steps

  • Take a bowl and add one cup of low-fat yogurt to it.
  • Add 2 tablespoons of crushed flax seeds to the bowl.
  • Then, add half cup each of fresh strawberries and blueberries to the bowl.
  • Add a teaspoon of cinnamon and mix all the ingredients thoroughly.
  • Relish your snack. 

Flaxseed Trail Mix

Make yourself a healthy and nutritious breakfast by preparing your own flax seed trail mix. 

Essentials Required

  • Walnuts
  • Flax seeds
  • Raw honey
  • Dried figs
  • Cinnamon powder

Processing Time

15 minutes

Steps

  • Take a bowl and a cup of walnuts to it.
  • Add a cup of dried figs and 2 tablespoons of crushed flax seeds into the bowl.
  • Then, add half a cup of raw honey to the mix.
  • Put a teaspoon of cinnamon powder and mix the ingredients well. 
  • Preheat the oven to 190 degrees.
  • Transfer the trail mix to a baking tray and bake it for about 10-12 minutes. 
  • Let the trail mix cool down and then transfer the contents to an airtight jar. 
  • Enjoy the trail mix as a post-workout treat or give it to your kids as an evening snack.

This healthy snack might be your key to achieving healthy and beautiful hair.

Potential Flax Seeds Side Effects to Consider

Apart from numerous benefits, there are some probable flax seeds' side effects on hair and body that you must keep in mind.

  • Flax seeds are infused with properties of being a good laxative, thus are beneficial for managing constipation. But too much consumption of seeds could cause stomach discomfort, diarrhea, and irregular bowel movements (11).
  • A high intake of flax seeds might cause intestinal damage. 
  • Flaxseed consumption is known to lower blood pressure. So, while flaxseeds can feel like a godsend remedy for high blood pressure patients, they might prove fatal for those dealing with low blood pressure (12).
  • Flaxseed is known to increase estrogen levels, which can lead to hormonal changes (13). So, pregnant and breastfeeding women should avoid consumption of flaxseeds. 
  • Flaxseeds might interact with certain blood thinners and medication for diabetes. 
  • There’s always a chance of you being allergic to flaxseeds. If you notice any redness, swelling, skin irritation, dryness, itchiness, etc on ingesting flaxseeds or their topical application, seek advice from a medical practitioner immediately.
